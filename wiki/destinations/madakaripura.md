# Madakaripura Waterfall

## Entity Summary (40-word, SSOT §9_4)

Madakaripura is Java's tallest waterfall in Probolinggo, East Java. A narrow canyon gorge leads to a horseshoe curtain waterfall fed from multiple directions. Historical context: said to be the final meditation site of Gajah Mada, Majapahit prime minister. Easy ~20 min trek from roadside parking.

## Why Visitors Come

**Narrow canyon approach** — rock walls close in as you walk the ~1.5 km gorge. Anticipation builds.

**Horseshoe curtain** — water enters the basin from multiple directions across a curved cliff face. Visitors inevitably get wet from spray and falling water; raincoat strongly recommended.

**Mythological significance** — said to be the final meditation site of Gajah Mada, the 14th-century Majapahit prime minister. The site retains spiritual significance in local culture.

## Logistics

- Canyon walk: ~1.5 km one-way; ~20 min from roadside parking (SSOT) — terrain is wet and slippery in places
- Raincoat strongly recommended; JVTO guides brief in advance
- Horse option available for guests preferring not to walk the canyon stretch
- Near Probolinggo — natural same-day combo with Bromo
