# Tumpak Sewu Waterfall

## Entity Summary (40-word, SSOT §9_4)

Tumpak Sewu is a multi-tiered canyon waterfall (~120 m) in Lumajang, East Java. Nicknamed "Java's Niagara" for its curtain of streams cascading over a curved cliff face into a canyon. Accessed via rim viewpoint or steep canyon descent (~300 steps).

## Two Vantage Points

1. **Rim viewpoint** — panoramic view of the full curtain, short walk from parking. Suitable for guests not descending.
2. **Base canyon** — steep descent (~300 steps, ropes in places, guides needed). Guests stand at the base of the falls, surrounded by mist and noise.

JVTO guides assess fitness on the day and brief the descent before starting. Decision to descend or stay at the rim is made guest-side.
