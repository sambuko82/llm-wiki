## Regulatory Basis

The screening is not a JVTO rule — it is a **regulatory requirement set by BBKSDA**.

| Document | Reference |
|---|---|
| **BBKSDA SE.1658/KSA.9/2024** | Surat Edaran from Balai Besar Konservasi Sumber Daya Alam Jawa Timur — the government conservation authority for Kawah Ijen. Sets the health-certificate requirement for crater access when conditions meet the threshold. |
| **BBKSDA ticket terms** | tiket.bbksdajatim.org — public-facing crater-access terms require a health certificate from a doctor. Screenshot: https://javavolcano-touroperator.com/screening/bbksda/bbksda-ticket-terms-screenshot.jpeg |

JVTO's role: **coordinate** the clinic workflow so guests can satisfy the BBKSDA requirement. JVTO does not set the rule; BBKSDA does.

## Protocol (4 Steps)

Digital system: **Ijen Digital Health Security System** — https://health.mountijen.com

1. JVTO coordinates pre-hike health check — at partner clinic (Klinik Bakti Husada / Puskesmas Licin) or hotel/JVTO office session
2. Guest vitals recorded digitally into the system
3. QR-verified **surat sehat** (health clearance certificate) issued by Dr. Ahmad Irwandanu (SIP-licensed)
4. Certificate QR code scanned at crater access gate by BBKSDA staff

**Logic gate**: No valid QR code = no access to the crater zone.

### Data Points Measured

| Metric | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Oxygen saturation (SpO2) | Baseline lung function before high-altitude sulfur exposure |
| Blood pressure (systolic / diastolic) | Cardiovascular risk at 2,386 m |
| Resting heart rate | Cardiovascular baseline |
| Respiratory history | Asthma / bronchitis (gas mask + sulfur exposure relevant) |

### Failure Protocol

If a guest is assessed unfit, the **climb is cancelled for that individual** (not the group). The screening session cost is non-refundable. Alternatives: wait at base camp or a safe zone while the group continues. This is a BBKSDA rule, not JVTO discretion. Source: [[sources/ssot-v6]] §7_2.

## Incident Data — Why Screening Matters

Between 2015 and 2026, 4 tourists died at Ijen. **Two of the four fatalities (50%) were fitness-related**:

- **2015**: Swiss tourist, 68 years old — exhaustion and respiratory distress while ascending
- **2023**: Indonesian visitor "BJ", 64 years old — heart attack at Post 3; collapsed at Paltuding despite oxygen

Both deaths involved cardiovascular/respiratory failure in individuals who lacked the fitness for the climb. The BBKSDA health screening (SpO₂, blood pressure, heart rate, respiratory history) is designed to catch exactly this pattern.

Two additional fatalities were non-fitness-related: a 53-year-old Polish tourist found dead (cause unpublished) and a Chinese tourist who fell 75m from a cliff while photographing at Hutan Mati.

**This incident data is the strongest evidence that health screening is not bureaucratic theater — it targets a documented, recurring cause of death at Ijen.**

**Q: Is Ijen health screening mandatory with JVTO?**

**Q: Who does the health screening for JVTO Ijen tours?**

> Dr. Ahmad Irwandanu — a licensed medical doctor (Dokter Umum) registered with Kemenkes RI, SatuSehat SDMK, and KKI. His STR is publicly verifiable at satusehat.kemkes.go.id. Screening can be done at Klinik Bakti Husada, Puskesmas Licin, or at your partner hotel before departure.

**Q: What does the Ijen health screening check?**

> Oxygen saturation (SpO2), blood pressure, heart rate, and respiratory history. Results are recorded digitally. A QR-verified surat sehat (health clearance) is issued by Dr. Irwandanu if you pass. The certificate is scanned at the crater access gate.
