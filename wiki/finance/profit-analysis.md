**Key observations:**
- **Best margin**: 1D Bromo Sunrise (34.9%) — short trip, low COGS, high perceived value.

- **Sweet spot**: 3D–4D packages from Surabaya cluster at 25–29% margin.

## Cost Driver Analysis

### What dominates COGS?

**1-day packages**: Transport + crew + activities dominate. No accommodation cost.
- 1D Bromo Sunrise (1 pax): Vehicle 250k + Crew 250k + Fuel 200k + Bromo ticket 160k + Jeep 500k = ~1.36M of 1.545M total (88% from transport/activities).

**Multi-day packages (3–5 days)**: Accommodation becomes the largest single cost category.
- On a 3D package, 2 nights of accommodation at mid-tier (450–700k/night) = 900k–1.4M, often 30–40% of per-pax variable costs.
- The 3D Taman Safari package has the highest per-pax COGS (3,315,000 for 2 pax) because Baobab Safari Resort rooms start at 1,500,000/night — the accommodation alone exceeds total COGS of budget 3D packages.

**Ferry-inclusive routes** (Bali to/from Sby): Ferry ticket (16k/pax) is cheap, but ferry guide (100k/unit) + extra travel time + additional fuel adds ~300k+ to the route cost. The 5D Bali route has 1M higher COGS than the equivalent Sby route.

### Cost category breakdown (typical 3D package, 2 pax, mid-tier hotel)

| Category | Estimated Cost | % of COGS |
|----------|---------------|-----------|

## Pax Count Impact

Group size has a dramatic effect on per-pax COGS because fixed costs (vehicle, crew, fuel, tolls) are divided across the group.

| Pax Count | Example Package (4D Ijen-Papuma-Tumpak-Bromo Sby) | COGS/Pax | vs. 1-pax |
|-----------|------------------------------------------------------|----------|-----------|
| 1 | — (extrapolated) | ~7,000,000 | baseline |
| 4 | 4D Ijen-Bromo-Mada Sby | 2,818,750 | -60% |
| 11 | 4D Ijen-Papuma-Tumpak-Bromo Sby | 2,332,863 | -67% |

**Fixed cost per day** (vehicle + crew + fuel + tolls): ~700k–1.3M/day depending on vehicle type.
- For a 4-day trip: ~2.8M–5.2M in fixed costs.
- 1 pax absorbs all of it. 11 pax split it to ~255k–473k each.

**Implication for pricing**: Solo travelers must be priced much higher per person, but still represent a lower total booking value. Group bookings are more efficient but require appropriate vehicle upgrades (Hiace at 1.1M/day vs MPV at 250k/day).

## Optimization Opportunities

### 1. Hotel Tier Selection
The largest controllable cost lever. Switching from Baobab Safari Resort (1.5M+/night) to a mid-tier hotel (450–700k/night) saves 800k–1M per night. For a 2-night package, this is 1.6M–2M in savings — enough to shift a 22% margin to 35%+.

**Recommendation**: Offer 2–3 hotel tiers (Budget/Standard/Premium) on the website with transparent pricing, as this directly affects package cost by 20–40%.

### 2. Bromo Jeep Sharing for Groups
Bromo Jeep costs 500,000/unit (seats 4–5). For groups of 8+, 2 jeeps cost 1M. The student jeep rate (450k) is available for student groups. Groups of 4 sharing one jeep vs. 2 couples in 2 jeeps saves 500k per visit.

### 3. Route Efficiency (Sby vs. Bali)
Surabaya-start routes consistently have lower COGS than Bali-start equivalents:
- 5D Sby: 6,831,500 vs. 5D Bali: 7,836,000 (difference: 1,004,500)
- The Bali route adds ferry costs, extra fuel, and typically an additional night of accommodation.

**Recommendation**: Promote Surabaya-start packages as the default. Position Bali-start as a convenience upgrade with appropriate pricing premium.

### 4. Crew Deployment
Using a Driver cum Guide (250k/day) instead of Driver (200k) + Escort Guide (250k) saves 200k/day. For a 4-day trip, this is 800k savings. Suitable for simple 1–2 day itineraries but not for complex multi-destination tours where a dedicated guide adds value.

### 5. Meal Bundling
Internal meals (Riverside 65k, Baratha 60k, Kecombrang 50k) are significantly cheaper than ad-hoc tourist restaurant meals. Pre-bundling meals at partner venues controls costs and improves guest experience.
