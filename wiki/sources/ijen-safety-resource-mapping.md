# Mount Ijen Safety and Support Resource Mapping

*Structured safety resource summary with 5 context categories and 14 source references. Extracted from Excel spreadsheet.*

---

## Summary

A consolidated mapping of safety hazards, support systems, and regulatory controls at Kawah Ijen. Covers 5 categories: physical hazards, health screening, Tourist Police support, Ijen Rijik closure program, and guide supervision.

## Key Extractions

### 1. Safety Hazards

| Hazard | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Terrain | Extreme steep slopes, cliff-side fall risks during photography, potential landslides on crater walls |
| Gas exposure | Toxic sulfur dioxide (SO₂) — mandatory gas masks |
| Temperature | Freezing temps reaching 2°C |
| Restricted zone | Hutan Mati (Dead Forest) area — restricted after April 2024 fatality |
| Crater radius | Stay within 500m radius of crater |
| Warning system | Red flag warnings — obey at all times |

Required equipment: gas masks, flashlights (headlamps), cold-weather clothing.

### 2. Health Screening

Mandatory digital health checks including blood pressure and vital signs recording. Screening ensures fitness to climb and prevents incidents related to heart conditions or asthma. Corroborates existing BBKSDA SE.1658/KSA.9/2024 data in [[credentials/medical-screening]].

### 3. Tourist Police Safety Support (Pam Obvit + Patwal)

Direct safety supervision and escorts by Tourist Police (Pam Obvit) and Traffic Police (Patwal). Evidence: SPRIN-POLPAR and SPRIN-WAL-TRAVEL documents. Official police briefings for guides and miners. Status: Active operational support.

Corroborates [[credentials/police-integration]] evidence chain.

### 4. Ijen Rijik Monthly Closure

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Program name | Ijen Rijik |
| Purpose | Ecosystem cleaning and recovery |
| Schedule | Every first Friday of month, 00:00–23:59 WIB |
| Scope | Total closure — all tourism AND mining activities |
| Established | March 1, 2019 |
| Status | Active scheduled event |

New detail: program named "Ijen Rijik," established March 1, 2019. Updates [[website/operational-facts]] closure section.

### 5. Guide Supervision and Cliff Safety

Mandatory supervision by APGI-certified guides in steep cliffs and toxic gas zones. Green briefings for waste management and cliff safety conducted for all guides. Applies especially to inexperienced hikers and international tourists.
