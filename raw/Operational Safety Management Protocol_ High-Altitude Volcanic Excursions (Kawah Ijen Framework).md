# **Operational Safety Management Protocol: High-Altitude Volcanic Excursions (Kawah Ijen Framework)**

## **1\. Regulatory Foundation and Strategic Mandate**

In high-altitude volcanic environments, operational certainty is not a product of environmental predictability, but of disciplined, standardized risk management. This protocol establishes that safety is a core operational discipline, requiring rigorous adherence to the "Authority Shield." Strategic mandate is built upon the synthesis of legal compliance and the mitigation of physical hazards.

### **The Authority Shield: Mandatory Legal Artifacts**

Operations within the Kawah Ijen volcanic complex are strictly regulated by **BBKSDA Jawa Timur** (Balai Besar Konservasi Sumber Daya Alam), specifically under the jurisdiction of **RKW 18** and **RKW 15** Kawah Ijen. The following artifacts are mandatory for all professional operators to verify operational legitimacy:

* **Business Identification Number (NIB):** 1102230032918 (Registered corporate liability).  
* **Tourism Business License (TDUP):** Specific authorization for Travel Agency and Tour Guiding services.  
* **Company Registry (AHU):** AHU-0023020 (Publicly verifiable corporate identity).  
* **Official Sector Approval:** Recognized membership in the **HPWKI** (Ijen Special Tourism Actors Association).  
* **Surat Edaran (SE) Compliance:** Adherence to the most recent mandates, specifically **SE.1658/K2/BIDTEK.1/KSA/9/2024**, which governs the reopening and SOPs for the TWA Kawah Ijen.

Regulatory compliance is the primary barrier against the physical hazards inherent in the 2,386m ASL volcanic environment.

## **2\. Historical Incident Analysis: The "Lessons Learned" Framework**

Proactive safety engineering utilizes historical data—specifically falls, respiratory failures, and transit accidents—as a diagnostic tool to eliminate systemic vulnerabilities.

### **Critical Incident Log: Engineering Safety Boundaries**

The root causes identified—ranging from wardrobe choices to unmanaged hypertension—demonstrate that most fatalities are failures of pre-ascent screening and personnel discipline.

## **3\. Mandatory Personnel Standards: Guide Competency and Discipline**

The human element (guides and drivers) represents the final fail-safe in high-risk zones. Technical competency must be paired with a "Police-Led Safety Mindset," as modeled by the **JVTO** leadership under Agung Sambuko (Mr. Sam), an active Tourist Police officer.

### **Tactical Personnel Standards**

* **The "Lamborghini Ijen" Evacuation Standard:** Use of the traditional trolley for medical evacuation requires a minimum of **three personnel to pull uphill** and **two personnel to stabilize downhill** to ensure guest safety during transport.  
* **Professional Driver Certification:** Required for all transit through "High-Risk Braking Zones" like Sengkan Gandrung.

### **Mandatory Guide Behaviors**

1. **"Green Briefings":** Mandatory safety and environmental orientations pre-ascent.  
2. **The 500-Meter Hazard Exclusion Zone:** A non-negotiable radius limit from the crater floor, as recommended by the PVMBG.  
3. **Navigational Discipline:** Maintaining absolute group cohesion to prevent separation. Failure in this regard (as seen in the 2026 Muhammad Dzikri Maulana SAR case) is a critical breach of protocol.  
4. **Authority Integration:** Coordination with **Pam Obvit** (Tourist Police) and adherence to the SPRIN (Police Assignment) framework.

Personnel readiness is the necessary precursor to the deployment of physical safety tools.

## **4\. Gear Requirements and Digital Health Screening**

Secondary defense layers consist of high-grade personal protective equipment (PPE) and rigorous physiological auditing.

### **Ijen Digital Health Screening**

To prevent the cardiac and respiratory distress outcomes seen in the 2015 Swiss and 2024 Polish cases, a three-step pre-ascent barrier is mandatory:

1. **Vitals Assessment:** Measurement of blood pressure and heart rate.  
2. **Digital Logging:** Formal recording of data to identify high-risk individuals (hypertension/heart disease).  
3. **Clearance Issuance:** Mandatory "Surat Sehat" (Health Letter) required for entry at the Paltuding gate.

## **5\. Emergency Response and Tactical Incident Management**

Coordinated Search and Rescue (SAR) infrastructure is vital for managing incidents at Kawah Ijen. Tactical management relies on rapid mobilization and technical search methodologies.

### **Tactical SAR Response Protocol**

Based on the recovery of **Muhammad Dzikri Maulana (2026)**, the following chronological protocol is triggered by an incident:

* **Initial Report:** Immediate notification to the **TWA Kawah Ijen Call Center: 0812 2018 0930**.  
* **SRU Division:** Formation of Search and Rescue Units (BKSDA, TNI, Polri, HPWKI).  
* **Technical Search:** Initial "Hasty Search" of the 3.6km trail, followed by a **Type II Open Grid search** covering a 1km² area around the last known position.  
* **Technology Deployment:** Use of thermal drones for cliff-side (tebing) signatures.  
* **Evacuation:** Tactical transport via trolley to the Licin Health Center (Puskesmas).

### **The Risk Flag System**

* **Green:** Normal/Safe.  
* **Yellow:** Caution (Alert for gas/trail instability).  
* **Red:** High Risk—**Mask usage is mandatory** due to increased gas concentration; area closures may be imminent.

## **7\. Environmental Continuity: The "Ijen Rijik" Integration**

Operational safety is inextricably linked to the mitigation of **anthropogenic disturbance** (noise, light, and waste pollution) through the **Ijen Rijik** (Clean Ijen) program.

### **The Friday Closure Mandate**

On the **first Friday of every month**, TWA Kawah Ijen is closed to all tourism and mining for 24 hours (00:00–23:59 WIB). This mandate serves three functions:

1. **Ecosystem Recovery:** Reducing anthropogenic disturbance to allow for the natural movement of local wildlife and the recovery of the RKW 18/15 habitat.  
2. **Systemic Pembersihan (Cleanup):** Coordinated removal of waste (100–150kg monthly) from Paltuding to the crater rim by BKSDA, SAR, and HPWKI.  
3. **Proactive Restoration:** Targeted planting of endemic **Cemara Gunung** (*Casuarina junghuhniana*) to stabilize trail slopes and prevent erosion.

**Summary:** Safety at Kawah Ijen is a synthesis of historical awareness, regulatory discipline, and local expertise. Adherence to this protocol ensures that operational certainty remains the priority in an inherently uncertain landscape.
