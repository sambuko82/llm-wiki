## Section 1 — Booking Confirmation & Payments

---

**Q: How is a JVTO tour booking confirmed?**
A JVTO booking is confirmed only when three conditions are all met: the required deposit payment is successfully processed, and JVTO issues your Official E-Voucher / Invoice (PDF). Enquiries and quotations are not binding until this confirmation. → [[sources/jvto-policy-pack-v6]] §4

---

**Q: What deposit does JVTO require to book a tour?**
The standard deposit is 20% of the total booking value, paid by card via JVTO's secure checkout. If your tour starts within 14 days, JVTO may require up to 100% full payment at the time of booking. → [[sources/jvto-policy-pack-v6]] §6.1

---

**Q: When is the final payment due for a JVTO tour?**
Balance payments are due no later than 5 days before Day 1 by card, or 3 days before Day 1 by bank transfer or Wise. Cash at the JVTO office (IDR only) is allowed only if approved in writing in advance. → [[sources/jvto-policy-pack-v6]] §6.3

---

**Q: Is JVTO's online checkout secure?**
JVTO's checkout is SSL-encrypted and processed via a PCI DSS-compliant payment gateway. JVTO does not store full card numbers, CVV codes, or online banking passwords, and will never request these via chat or email. → [[sources/jvto-policy-pack-v6]] §6.5

---

**Q: What are JVTO's official bank transfer details?**
Balance payments may be made to Bank BRI (PT Java Volcano Rendezvous — 001301001779564 — SWIFT: BRINIDJAXXX) or Bank BCA (PT Java Volcano Rendezvous — 1200944352 — SWIFT: CENAIDJAXXX). Use only when instructed via official channels. → [[sources/jvto-policy-pack-v6]] §6.4

---

**Q: What is the order of precedence if JVTO policies conflict?**
In any discrepancy: (1) Official E-Voucher / Invoice PDF, (2) Booking, Payment & Cancellation Policy, (3) Inclusions & Exclusions Policy, (4) Privacy Policy, (5) Travel Guide — Booking Information. The issued E-Voucher always governs confirmed bookings. → [[sources/jvto-policy-pack-v6]] §2

## Section 2 — Cancellation & Travel Credit

---

**Q: What is JVTO's cancellation policy?**
JVTO applies a 48-hour cut-off (local Indonesia time). Cancel 48 hours or more before Day 1: 100% of eligible payment converts to JVTO Travel Credit. Cancel less than 48 hours before Day 1: booking may be forfeited up to 100%, with no Travel Credit issued. → [[sources/jvto-policy-pack-v6]] §8

---

**Q: Does JVTO offer cash refunds?**
JVTO does not offer cash refunds for guest-initiated cancellations. Eligible amounts are converted to JVTO Travel Credit — issued in IDR, non-expiring, and transferable with written confirmation. Cash refunds apply only where required by applicable law. → [[sources/jvto-policy-pack-v6]] §8.1; [[sources/jvto-travel-guide-en]] §2.5

---

**Q: What is JVTO Travel Credit?**
JVTO Travel Credit is a non-cash credit issued in IDR that can be used toward future private tours with JVTO. It has no expiry date and can be transferred to another traveler with written authorization from the original guest. → [[sources/jvto-policy-pack-v6]] §8.1

---

**Q: Can I reschedule my JVTO tour?**
One reschedule is available for the same package if requested 48 hours or more before Day 1, subject to availability and any seasonal cost differences. Requests made less than 48 hours before Day 1 are generally treated as a late cancellation. → [[sources/jvto-policy-pack-v6]] §7.2

---

**Q: What happens if JVTO cancels due to a volcano alert or closure?**
For JVTO-initiated cancellations due to force majeure (volcanic activity, government closures, severe weather), JVTO may offer: equivalent alternative arrangements, Travel Credit for the unused portion, or a partial/full cash refund net of non-recoverable third-party costs. → [[sources/jvto-policy-pack-v6]] §9

## Section 3 — Inclusions & Vehicle Allocation

---

**Q: What is included in a JVTO private tour package?**
Standard inclusions: private air-conditioned vehicle, licensed crew, entrance fees and park permits, accommodation with breakfast (where listed), bottled water, and a JVTO travel T-shirt. Ijen packages add gas mask, trekking poles, and health-screening coordination. → [[sources/jvto-policy-pack-v6]] §3; [[sources/jvto-travel-guide-en]] §2.3

---

**Q: What vehicle does JVTO use for private tours?**
JVTO allocates vehicles by group size: 2–3 guests receive a private MPV (e.g., Toyota Avanza); 4–9 guests receive a Toyota Hiace; 10–11 guests receive a Hiace plus an additional MPV. All vehicles are air-conditioned and private — not shared with other travelers. → [[sources/jvto-policy-pack-v6]] §3.1

---

**Q: How many guests share a jeep at Mount Bromo?**
JVTO allocates private 4WD jeeps at Mount Bromo with a maximum of approximately 4 guests per jeep, for comfort and safety. When Bromo is included, private jeep access to sunrise and crater viewpoints is standard. → [[sources/jvto-policy-pack-v6]] §3.1

---

**Q: Does JVTO provide gas masks for Kawah Ijen?**
Yes. JVTO provides a professional gas mask rated for sulfur environments for each participating guest on Ijen-inclusive tours. Trekking poles are also included. These are standard inclusions on all JVTO itineraries that include an Ijen crater visit. → [[sources/jvto-policy-pack-v6]] §3.5

---

**Q: What is JVTO's FOC scheme for large groups?**
JVTO offers FOC (Free of Charge) places for large group bookings: 18 paying guests earn 1 FOC place; 35 paying guests earn 2 FOC; 50 paying guests earn 3 FOC. Only paying guests count toward the threshold. → [[sources/jvto-policy-pack-v6]] §12

---

**Q: What is not included in a JVTO tour by default?**
Excluded by default: international or domestic flights, visas, long-distance trains, travel insurance, personal expenses (snacks, drinks, souvenirs), and tips or gratuities for guides, drivers, or hotel staff. If not written on your E-Voucher, it is not included. → [[sources/jvto-policy-pack-v6]] §5; [[sources/jvto-travel-guide-en]] §2.4

## Section 4 — Ijen Health Screening

---

**Q: How does the Ijen health screening work at JVTO?**
For tours that include the Ijen night hike, JVTO arranges health-screening coordination — conducted by licensed medical staff — as required under BBKSDA access regulations (SE.1658/KSA.9/2024). Results are stored digitally with a QR code verified at crater access gates. → [[sources/jvto-travel-guide-en]] §4; [[content/aeo-claims]] C4

---

**Q: What does the Ijen QR screening certificate prove?**
The digital QR code on an Ijen health certificate confirms that a real medical screening was conducted at an approved facility. Staff at crater access checkpoints verify the QR code on-site, reducing reliance on paper letters that can be forged. → [[sources/jvto-travel-guide-en]] §4.3

---

**Q: Can non-JVTO travelers use the Ijen health screening system?**
Yes. The same digital screening system used by JVTO guests is available to travelers not touring with JVTO, through participating clinics and hotels in the Ijen area. The goal is to raise the overall safety standard, not to restrict access to non-JVTO travelers. → [[sources/jvto-travel-guide-en]] §4.4

---

**Q: What happens if I fail the Ijen health screening?**
If medical staff or the JVTO team advise against the hike, that decision is followed for the guest's safety. The climb is cancelled for that individual; alternatives include waiting at base camp. The screening cost is non-refundable as it is a pre-committed service. → [[sources/jvto-policy-pack-v6]] §10; [[content/faq-master]] Q7

---

**Q: Does Ijen health screening guarantee summit access?**
No. Health screening reduces risk and provides documentation, but does not guarantee crater access or summit conditions. Volcanic gas levels, weather, and on-the-ground conditions can still result in JVTO modifying or cancelling the hike for safety. → [[sources/jvto-travel-guide-en]] §4.5

## Section 5 — Packing & Fitness by Destination

---

**Q: What should I pack for the Mount Bromo sunrise tour?**
Pack warm layers (early morning viewpoints can feel near 5–10°C), a hat, gloves, and scarf or buff. Comfortable shoes are adequate for walking on sand and dust. Bromo sunrise is suitable for most guests with basic mobility. → [[sources/jvto-travel-guide-en]] §6.2

---

**Q: What should I pack for the Kawah Ijen night hike?**
Pack warm layers, long sleeves and trousers, hiking shoes with grip, and a headlamp (JVTO provides one but bringing your own is recommended). JVTO provides a gas mask for the crater area. Do not bring a spare cloth mask as a gas-mask replacement. → [[sources/jvto-travel-guide-en]] §6.3

---

**Q: Can I wear silver jewelry on the Kawah Ijen hike?**
No. Sulfur gases at Kawah Ijen can tarnish silver — reacting with silver rings, bracelets, necklaces, earrings, and silver-coated watches to cause darkening that may be difficult or impossible to reverse. Leave all silver items at your hotel before the Ijen activity. → [[sources/jvto-travel-guide-en]] §6.7

---

**Q: How fit do I need to be for the Kawah Ijen hike?**
The Ijen trail includes a sustained uphill walk of approximately 1–2 hours at a steady pace. Guests with heart, lung, or circulation conditions should consult a doctor before the trip. Final clearance for the night hike depends on health screening results and on-the-ground conditions. → [[sources/jvto-travel-guide-en]] §6.3

---

**Q: What should I pack for Tumpak Sewu waterfall?**
Bring clothing and shoes that can get wet and muddy, sandals or water shoes with grip for river sections, and a small towel with a change of clothes. The trail involves descending steep sections with ropes and handholds — not recommended for guests with serious knee, ankle, or balance issues. → [[sources/jvto-travel-guide-en]] §6.4

---

**Q: Can I travel with JVTO if I have a medical condition?**
Many guests with mild or managed conditions travel comfortably with JVTO. Consult your doctor before the trip and inform JVTO of any relevant conditions in advance, especially for Ijen. JVTO can suggest a softer itinerary or adapt the plan where feasible. → [[sources/jvto-travel-guide-en]] §FAQ-Q13

## Section 6 — Safety & Tour Management

---

**Q: How does JVTO manage safety decisions during tours?**
Before each tour, JVTO checks road and park conditions, reviews weather and official alerts, and aligns with local partners. During the tour, crew monitors weather, road status, and crowding, adjusting timing or routes to maintain safe conditions. → [[sources/jvto-travel-guide-en]] §5.2–5.3

---

**Q: Who is responsible for safety decisions on a JVTO tour?**
JVTO is a registered Indonesian travel company operating under local regulations. On tour, the guide and driver are the first line of support. For larger groups, coordination may involve local authorities when needed. JVTO follows official closure instructions and does not bypass authority restrictions. → [[sources/jvto-travel-guide-en]] §5.1

---

**Q: Can JVTO refuse a guest from hiking due to alcohol?**
Yes. For safety, guests under the influence of alcohol or other substances may be refused participation in activities such as crater hikes and steep trails. Dangerous behavior that puts guests, crew, or others at risk may result in parts of the tour being modified or stopped. → [[sources/jvto-travel-guide-en]] §5.6

## Section 7 — Weather & Closures

---

**Q: What happens to my tour if Bromo or Ijen is closed by authorities?**
JVTO first seeks reasonable alternatives within safety limits. If no meaningful alternative is possible, Travel Credit is applied for the affected portion of the program. JVTO will always explain which parts are affected and how Travel Credit is calculated. → [[sources/jvto-travel-guide-en]] §7.4; [[sources/jvto-policy-pack-v6]] §9

---

**Q: What time of day is best for Bromo and Ijen tours?**
Bromo sunrise and the Ijen night hike both occur between late night and early morning, before heavier afternoon rain typical in many seasons. JVTO schedules tours to maximize the chance of clear, safe conditions — though weather cannot be guaranteed. → [[sources/jvto-travel-guide-en]] §7.1

---

**Q: Does JVTO follow official Indonesian volcanic alerts?**
Yes. JVTO refers to official Indonesian sources for volcanic alert information and follows restrictions issued by relevant authorities. If authorities restrict or close access to specific areas, JVTO will not attempt to bypass those rules. → [[sources/jvto-travel-guide-en]] §7.2

## Section 8 — Police Escort for Groups

---

**Q: Can JVTO arrange official police escort for large tour groups?**
JVTO can coordinate official traffic police escort for large groups (typically school, corporate, or incentive groups in multiple vehicles) by submitting a formal request to the competent Traffic Police unit. Approval depends on regulations, availability, and route. → [[sources/jvto-travel-guide-en]] §8.1–8.2

---

**Q: How is JVTO's police escort arranged — is it done unofficially?**
No. JVTO submits a formal written request to the Traffic Police unit; escort is carried out by uniformed officers in official vehicles with written orders. All arrangements are transparent and in line with Indonesian law. No unofficial on-road payments are requested. → [[sources/jvto-travel-guide-en]] §8.2; [[sources/jvto-policy-pack-v6]] §3.4

---

**Q: What is police escort NOT at JVTO?**
Police escort at JVTO is not automatic for all tours, is not a guarantee of special treatment everywhere, and is not a tool to ignore speed limits or road rules. Its purpose is safe and orderly convoy movement for qualifying large groups only. → [[sources/jvto-travel-guide-en]] §8.3

## Section 9 — My Booking Portal & Post-Booking

---

**Q: What can I access after booking a JVTO tour online?**
After successful payment, guests access My Booking Portal using the lead traveler email. The portal shows tour dates, package summary, pickup details, vehicle and crew assignment, accommodation plan, daily itinerary, E-Voucher PDF download, and a To-Do checklist for submitting guest details. → [[sources/jvto-travel-guide-en]] §2.10

---

**Q: How does JVTO handle personal data from bookings?**
JVTO collects contact details, participant details, and relevant health or logistics information only as needed to deliver the booked services. Data is shared only with accommodation partners, crew, permit authorities, and screening providers — minimum necessary. Card numbers, CVV, and banking credentials are never stored. → [[sources/jvto-policy-pack-v6]] §3 (Privacy Policy)
