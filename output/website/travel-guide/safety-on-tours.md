# Safety on Tours — How We Make Decisions

---

## Risk-Aware Planning, Not Risk Avoidance

Mr. Sam is a Tourist Police officer first, tour operator second. That order shapes every operational decision we make. Safety at JVTO is not a marketing position — it is a written framework applied before and during every tour. We do not remove guests from real environments; we prepare them for those environments with accurate information, trained guides, proper equipment, and written contingency procedures.

Guests consistently report pre-hike safety briefings as a defining feature of the JVTO experience. That is deliberate: every activity starts with a briefing because the alternative — assuming guests know what to expect — creates the conditions for preventable incidents.

---

## Plan-B Framework — Written Policy, Not a Promise

When conditions change, JVTO does not improvise. We operate a documented Plan-B framework for volcanic closures, severe weather, and access restrictions.

Before Day 1, we check road and park conditions, weather alerts, hotel and transport availability, and align with our local partners at each destination. If an alert or closure is issued — whether for volcanic activity at Bromo, gas conditions at Ijen, or flood risk at Madakaripura — we contact guests in advance, explain what has changed, and propose reasonable alternatives.

During the tour, guides monitor weather, road conditions, crowding, and local announcements in real time. We may leave viewpoints earlier, arrive later, switch routes, or choose safer paths. If a key element of your package cannot operate — for example, if Bromo's national park closes due to volcanic activity — the policy response is documented in your booking: guests are briefed, alternatives are offered, and Travel Credit is applied to unused portions where applicable.

No guest is left without a program. That is the policy, written down.

---

## Safety Briefings Before Each Activity

Before every hike, canyon descent, or jeep sequence, JVTO guides conduct a safety briefing. What to expect on the terrain, where to put your feet, when to hold the rope, what the gas mask covers and what it does not cover. Reviewers across multiple platforms name individual guides by name and cite these briefings specifically — Anjas, Taufik, Rendi, Gufron, Kiki.

This pattern maps to what JVTO calls T4 in its review analysis: guests repeatedly mention feeling safe, well-informed, and prepared. The briefing is not optional and not abbreviated for speed.

## BBKSDA-Supervised Guide Training at Paltuding

Ijen local guides at JVTO are HPWKI-affiliated (Himpunan Pelaku Wisata Khusus Ijen). HPWKI membership means completion of BBKSDA-supervised training — government-hosted, not self-certified.

In May 2024, BBKSDA Jawa Timur ran a three-day training programme (20–22 May 2024) at Paltuding — the Ijen trailhead and visitor centre inside the park. The programme targeted 250 HPWKI-affiliated guides and covered:

1. Conservation area awareness
2. Local guide responsibilities within Kawah Ijen Natural Tourism Park
3. SAR (Search and Rescue) techniques
4. PPGD — Emergency Medical Response (Penanggulangan Penderita Gawat Darurat)

This is the institutional training record for the guide team at Ijen. It was documented by BBKSDA Jawa Timur, not JVTO. The scope — SAR and emergency medical response — goes beyond standard guiding and positions guides as field extensions of park management.

## Gas Masks on Ijen Routes

JVTO provides professional gas masks as a standard inclusion on all Ijen-relevant packages. Sulphur dioxide concentrations at the Ijen crater floor fluctuate with gas venting, wind direction, and access-point proximity. The masks are fitted, not optional accessories, and are briefed before the crater descent begins.

A spare scarf or dust mask guests bring is not a substitute for the JVTO-provided gas mask in the crater area.

---

## Emergency Handling

For serious medical situations on tour, JVTO guides coordinate with local clinics, evacuation services, and — where relevant — Ditpamobvit contacts in the park area. Because all tours are private, the guide and driver respond only to your group; there is no divided attention across strangers.

JVTO strongly recommends that all guests carry valid travel insurance covering medical emergencies, evacuation, repatriation, trip interruption, and adventure activities including volcanic hiking and canyon descent. Insurance is not included in tour pricing.

Guests under the influence of alcohol or substances may be refused participation in hikes, crater descents, or steep trails — this is a written safety policy, not a discretionary call.
