# Private vs. Shared Volcano Tours: Why Execution Control Matters

When planning a tour to Mount Bromo and Mount Ijen, you will choose between a **100% private tour** and a **shared tour (open trip)**. While shared tours attract budget-conscious travelers with low base rates, they require significant compromises in safety, vehicle comfort, and schedule flexibility.

Here is an analysis of how a private JVTO tour compares to typical shared tours in East Java.

---

## 1. Key Comparison

| Feature | Private Tour (JVTO) | Shared Tour (Open Trip) |
|---|---|---|
| **Vehicle Occupancy** | Dedicated vehicle for your group only | Shared van with 10–14 strangers |
| **Pacing & Schedule** | Flexible. You decide when to stop, rest, or eat | Rigid. Fixed departure times; no adjustments |
| **Dedicated Guide** | Private guide assigned to your party | 1 guide responsible for 15–20 travelers |
| **Safety Integration** | Direct health-check coordination and individual gear checks | Mass screening at the trailhead; no individual tracking |
| **Pricing Model** | **All-inclusive**. Zero surprise payments. | Low entry price; hidden jeep or entry fees |
| **Rescheduling & Credit** | 100% Lifetime Travel Credit (if cancelled ≥48h before) | Non-refundable; immediate forfeiture |

---

## 2. Pacing Control: The Key to Volcanic Safety

Hiking active volcanoes requires physical endurance. Kawah Ijen's night ascent is a steep 3 km climb followed by a rocky descent into the sulfur crater.

- **On a Shared Tour**: The group must move at a uniform pace. If you need to rest, you risk being left behind by the guide who must keep the entire group on schedule to catch the sunrise.
- **On a Private JVTO Tour**: Your guide monitors your group’s breathing, heart rate, and fatigue. You can pause to rest, adjust your pace, or decide to skip the crater descent without affecting anyone else. Your safety is our primary focus.

---

## 3. Vehicle Comfort and Overland Fatigue

Bromo-Ijen routes require 4 to 6 hours of overland driving between cities like Surabaya, Probolinggo, Banyuwangi, and Malang.

- **On a Shared Tour**: Vans are packed to maximum capacity to cover operational costs. You will spend hours waiting at fixed pick-up points for other guests, and rest stops are strictly scheduled.
- **On a Private JVTO Tour**: Your group gets a dedicated AC vehicle. We allocate an MPV for 2–3 guests and a spacious Toyota Hiace for larger groups. You can request restroom breaks, stop for local food (like East Java durians), or pause to stretch whenever you choose.

---

## 4. Transparent Inclusions vs. Hidden Charges

A common complaint in shared tours is the "add-on fee" structure.

- **Shared tours** often exclude national park entrance fees (which cost up to IDR 220,000/person for foreign tourists on weekends), charge extra for gas mask rentals at Ijen, or require local cash payments to board the Bromo 4WD jeep.
- **JVTO packages** are transparently all-inclusive. Private AC transport, dedicated guide and driver, entrance fees, private 4WD Bromo jeep, safety gear (respirators and poles), medical screening coordination, daily water, and accommodation are bundled into the price. "Read the Rulebook Before You Book" is our policy.

---

## 5. Rescheduling and Cancellation Flexibility

Volcanic activity, flight delays, and personal health issues can disrupt travel plans.

- **Shared tours** generally operate a strict "no refund, no reschedule" policy because seats are sold individually and departures depend on filling the van.
- **JVTO's booking terms** provide industry-leading protection. If you cancel your tour at least 48 hours before Day 1, 100% of the eligible booking value is converted into a Lifetime Travel Credit. This credit has no expiration date, is fully transferable, and can be used to book any JVTO route. We also offer one free reschedule with 48 hours' notice.
