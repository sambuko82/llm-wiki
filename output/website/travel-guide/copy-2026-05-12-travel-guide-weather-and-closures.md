# Weather, Volcanic Activity & Closures

Bromo and Ijen are active volcanoes. Tumpak Sewu and Madakaripura are canyon environments where rainfall matters. Conditions change. This page explains what we watch, how we respond, and what you can expect if your dates are affected.

---

## Mount Bromo — Closures & Seasonal Patterns

Bromo sits inside the Bromo Tengger Semeru National Park (BBTNBTS). When volcanic alert levels rise, park management or BNPB may restrict or close crater access, the caldera floor, or the national park roads. JVTO follows those instructions without exception.

**Volcanic closure SOP**: If Bromo closes for volcanic activity during your booking window, JVTO activates its Plan-B framework — alternative viewpoints, rescheduled timing, or replacement destinations. Guests are briefed in advance. You will not arrive to find us improvising.

**Kasada festival**: The annual Tengger Hindu ceremony at Pura Luhur Poten on the caldera floor falls on a date that varies by lunar calendar each year. During Kasada, Bromo crater access may be restricted or heavily crowded. JVTO activates its Plan-B SOP for bookings that coincide with this period. If your dates are near a Kasada window, ask us when you enquire.

**Best season for Bromo**: Dry season, April–October. Pre-dawn temperatures at the Penanjakan viewpoint run 5–15°C (41–59°F). Main activities — sunrise at Penanjakan, the Sea of Sand jeep traverse, crater walk — happen in the pre-dawn window (03:00–05:00) before rain is more likely later in the day.

## Kawah Ijen — Gas Activity, Access Rules & Closures

Ijen is monitored by BBKSDA East Java (bbksdajatim.org) and the Indonesian volcanology authority (PVMBG/Magma Indonesia). Several types of restriction apply:

**Monthly closure**: The Ijen crater is closed to visitors on the **first Friday of every month**. This is a scheduled closure, not weather-dependent. If your Ijen hike falls on the first Friday of the month, JVTO will flag this when you enquire.

**Gas and safety closures**: BBKSDA issues additional closures based on volcanic gas levels and safety assessments. These are announced via bbksdajatim.org. JVTO monitors these in real time and will not send guests to a closed crater.

**Blue Fire visibility**: Blue Fire is a natural phenomenon subject to weather and gas activity. It is visible pre-dawn in suitable conditions at the crater floor. JVTO designs Ijen itineraries around a midnight departure to maximise the viewing window — but we cannot promise natural phenomena. Cloud cover, gas venting direction, and wind conditions are outside our control.

**Health certificate access rules**: Ijen crater access can require a recent local health certificate under BBKSDA SE.1658/KSA.9/2024. If access rules require it, JVTO coordinates the clinic workflow before the hike. No valid QR code means no crater zone access — this is an BBKSDA checkpoint requirement, not a JVTO policy.

**Best season for Ijen**: Dry season, April–October. Crater rim temperatures run 10–15°C (50–59°F) at the midnight departure window.

## Tumpak Sewu & Madakaripura — Rainfall & Canyon Conditions

Both Tumpak Sewu and Madakaripura are canyon and waterfall environments. Heavy rainfall can affect water levels, trail safety, and access.

- **Tumpak Sewu**: The canyon descent uses fixed ropes and handholds on steep, wet terrain. Heavy rainfall increases slip risk on the descent. Guides assess conditions on the day; if the route is unsafe, the guide makes the call.
- **Madakaripura**: The canyon approach requires wading through a stream. Water levels rise after heavy rain. The experience involves getting wet regardless of conditions — that is the nature of the site.

**Best season for both**: Visiting during the dry season (April–October) is generally more comfortable, but these are year-round destinations. Even in the wet season, many Tumpak Sewu and Madakaripura days proceed without issue.

## Seasonal Overview — East Java

| Season | Months | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Dry season | April – October | Recommended for Bromo and Ijen. Clearer skies, lower rainfall, better road conditions. |
| Wet season | November – March | Higher rainfall, possible cloud at viewpoints. Canyon destinations (Tumpak Sewu, Madakaripura) remain popular. |

## Plan-B — What JVTO Does When Conditions Change

If a key activity or destination is unavailable on your dates due to volcanic activity, official closure, or safety assessment:

1. We contact you in advance — you are not surprised on the day.
2. We explain what has changed and why.
3. We propose reasonable alternatives: different viewpoints, adjusted timing, replacement destinations.
4. If a key itinerary element cannot operate at all, Travel Credit applies to the unused portion per your booking terms.

JVTO does not bypass official restrictions. If BBKSDA, BBTNBTS, or BNPB closes a site, that closure applies to us regardless of your booking.

For the full written policy on closures, reschedules, and Travel Credit, see the [Booking, Payment & Cancellation Policy](https://javavolcano-touroperator.com/policy/booking-payment-cancellation).
