## Mount Bromo — What Level II Means for Your Tour

**Current restriction**: Do not enter within **1 km of the active crater** of Gunung Bromo.

Here is what is open and what is restricted at Level II Waspada:

| Area | Status |
|---|---|
| Penanjakan sunrise viewpoint (~5 km from crater) | ✅ Open — outside exclusion zone |
| Sea of Sand jeep traverse | ✅ Open — caldera floor approach |
| Crater rim walk | ⚠️ Restricted — within 1 km exclusion zone |
| Caldera floor descent toward crater base | ⚠️ Confirm with BBTNBTS park authority |

The Penanjakan sunrise — the main reason most guests visit Bromo — is not affected by the current Level II status. The 1 km restriction targets the crater rim approach, not the viewpoint.

**Phreatic risk**: At Level II, PVMBG specifically warns of sudden phreatic eruptions without prior symptoms. JVTO will not bring guests to any restricted zone while Level II is active.

**JVTO Plan-B at Level II**: If any booked itinerary segment falls inside the exclusion zone, JVTO activates its Plan-B framework — alternative viewpoints, adjusted routing, or replacement segments. You are briefed before departure, not on the day.

*Full restriction details: [MAGMA Indonesia Bromo Report](https://magma.esdm.go.id/v1/gunung-api/laporan/315742)*

## Kawah Ijen — Current Status

**Alert Level I — Normal.** Standard JVTO Ijen operations proceed without restriction.

**Permanent baseline conditions** at Ijen (these are normal, not elevated risk):
- Continuous micro-tremor is the standard hydrothermal baseline at this volcano
- Sulphur gas is present at the crater floor at all times — gas masks are standard JVTO equipment
- Crater lake access: descent to the lake floor is never permitted (regulatory, not alert-dependent)
- Camping within 500 m of the crater is not permitted (standard BBKSDA rule)

**Health screening**: BBKSDA SE.1658/KSA.9/2024 health-certificate requirements apply regardless of alert level — this is a regulatory rule, not triggered by elevated status. JVTO coordinates the screening when access rules require it. See [Ijen Health Screening](/travel-guide/ijen-health-screening).

**Monthly closure**: Ijen crater is closed on the **first Friday of every month** — this applies at any alert level.

## What the Alert Levels Mean

Indonesia's PVMBG uses a 4-level system for active volcanoes. Here is what each level means for JVTO tours:

| Level | Indonesian term | General meaning | JVTO operational status |
|---|---|---|---|
| Level I | Normal | Background volcanic activity | ✅ Standard operations — no change |
| Level II | Waspada | Elevated activity — increased monitoring | ⚠️ Exclusion zone enforced; Plan-B activates for restricted segments |
| Level III | Siaga | High activity — significant eruption risk | ❌ Tours to restricted zones suspended; Travel Credit issued |
| Level IV | Awas | Very high activity — eruption imminent or occurring | ❌ Full area suspension; Travel Credit issued |

JVTO does not make independent safety calls that override PVMBG, BBTNBTS, or BBKSDA directives. If an official body closes a site, that closure applies to JVTO guests.

## If Your Tour Dates Are Affected

**Level I or II**: Your tour proceeds. Level II adds restrictions on the crater rim approach. If your itinerary includes a restricted segment, JVTO contacts you to confirm the Plan-B arrangement before your arrival.

**Level III or IV**: JVTO contacts all affected guests directly. Travel Credit is issued for cancelled itinerary segments under the terms of the [Booking, Payment & Cancellation Policy](https://javavolcano-touroperator.com/policy/booking-payment-cancellation). You are not expected to monitor this yourself.

**How to ask**: If you have a confirmed booking and want a status update, message the JVTO WhatsApp at +62 822 4478 8833. You will receive a direct reply from the team, not an automated response.
