# Frequently Asked Questions

---

## General — Booking, Cancellation & Private Tours

### Are your tours private or shared?
All JVTO tours are private only. We do not mix bookings with other guests or sell seats in a shared coach. Your vehicle, driver, and guide are arranged for your group alone — no waiting for strangers, no compromises on pace.

### How do I book a JVTO tour?
The standard steps: (1) Send your dates, origin (Surabaya or Bali), group size, and preferred route to JVTO via WhatsApp (+62 822 4478 8833). (2) We send a tailored proposal with itinerary and price quotation. (3) You confirm; we issue a payment link. (4) After the deposit, you receive an Official E-Voucher with full trip details. (5) Private pick-up at your confirmed location on Day 1.

### How do deposits and payment deadlines work?
The standard deposit is 20% of the total booking value, paid by card via a secure JVTO checkout link. If Day 1 is within 14 days, up to 100% full payment may be required upfront. Card balance is due no later than 5 days before Day 1; bank wire or Wise balance no later than 3 days before Day 1.

### What is your cancellation policy?
JVTO uses a Travel Credit system rather than cash refunds for guest-initiated cancellations. Cancel 48 hours or more before Day 1 and 100% of eligible booking value converts to Lifetime Travel Credit — non-expiring, transferable with written authorisation, and usable on any future JVTO package. Cancel less than 48 hours before Day 1 and the booking is forfeited up to 100% with no Travel Credit. One free reschedule is available if requested 48 hours or more before Day 1, subject to availability.

### Do you offer cash refunds?
Cash refunds are not available for guest-initiated cancellations. Travel Credit is the standard outcome. For closures caused by volcanic activity, government restrictions, or force majeure, we may offer reasonable alternatives, Travel Credit for unused portions, or partial/full cash refund net of non-recoverable expenses — assessed case by case.

### What is included in the price?
All JVTO packages include: private air-conditioned vehicle, dedicated crew (driver-guide or driver + escort guide), all entrance fees and park permits, mineral water daily, hotel accommodation with breakfast, a JVTO travel T-shirt, and full pick-up to drop-off service. Bromo-inclusive packages add a private 4WD jeep. Ijen-relevant packages add gas masks, trekking poles, and health-screening coordination where BBKSDA rules require it. Bali-origin packages add Java-Bali ferry tickets. Helmets at Madakaripura and Tumpak Sewu are provided by local site management, not JVTO.

## Ijen

### How does the Ijen health screening work?
For tours that include the Ijen night hike, JVTO coordinates health-screening with licensed medical staff as required under BBKSDA access regulations (Surat Edaran SE.1658/KSA.9/2024). The aim is to reduce the risk of sending unfit guests into a demanding environment with sulphur gas and steep trails. Vitals measured include SpO2, blood pressure, heart rate, and respiratory history. The result carries a QR code verified by BBKSDA checkpoint staff at the crater access gate. No valid QR code means no crater zone access.

### Is the health screening mandatory?
Ijen access rules can require a recent local health certificate under BBKSDA SE.1658/KSA.9/2024. JVTO coordinates the clinic workflow when access rules require it. The requirement is regulatory — set by BBKSDA East Java — not unilaterally imposed by JVTO. The nurse visits your hotel the evening before the hike for the screening session.

### What happens if I do not pass the Ijen health screening?
If screening staff or the JVTO team advise that you should not attempt the hike, that decision is followed — it protects your safety and others on the trail. The climb is cancelled for that individual, not the group. Alternatives include waiting at base camp or a safe viewpoint area. The screening session cost is non-refundable. The rest of the group continues as planned.

### Can you guarantee we will see the Blue Fire?
No. Blue Fire is a natural phenomenon subject to weather and gas activity. JVTO designs itineraries around the best viewing window — the pre-dawn slot from midnight departure — but we cannot promise specific natural phenomena. Weather, cloud cover, and gas venting levels are outside our control.

### Does JVTO provide gas masks for Ijen?
Yes. Gas masks are a standard inclusion on all Ijen-relevant packages, provided by JVTO. This is separate from and independent of the health-screening process.

## Bromo

### What is included in the Bromo sequence?
All Bromo-relevant packages include a private 4WD jeep, pre-arranged before arrival — no on-the-day haggling. The jeep takes your group to the Penanjakan sunrise viewpoint (2,770 m), across the Sea of Sand caldera floor, and to the crater-rim drop-off. Maximum approximately 4 guests per jeep for comfort and safety.

### What happens if Bromo is closed on my dates?
We follow official instructions from Indonesian authorities and park management. Depending on the situation, we may adjust timings, use alternative viewpoints, replace destinations with safe alternatives, or activate our Plan-B procedure. Guests are briefed in advance — you will not be left without a program. The Kasada festival (annual Tengger Hindu ceremony, date varies by lunar calendar) can restrict Bromo access; JVTO activates its Plan-B SOP for bookings during this period.

### What are the temperatures at Bromo?
Pre-dawn temperatures at Bromo viewpoints typically run 5–15°C (41–59°F). Warm layers are essential — layering is more effective than a single thick garment.

## Madakaripura & Tumpak Sewu

### What should I expect at Madakaripura?
Madakaripura is a slot canyon with a narrow stream approach — you will wade through water and navigate wet stones. You will get wet in the canyon; that is part of the experience. Waterproof bags and a full change of clothes are essential. Helmets are provided at the canyon entrance by local site management.

### What should I expect at Tumpak Sewu?
Tumpak Sewu involves a steep canyon descent with fixed ropes and handholds on slippery ground, followed by a river traverse to reach the base of the falls. Water shoes with grip are strongly recommended. This route is not recommended for guests with serious knee, ankle, or balance issues.

## Packing & Fitness

### What fitness level do I need for each destination?
Bromo requires basic mobility — stepping in and out of a jeep, walking short distances on uneven volcanic sand. Ijen requires a sustained uphill walk of 1–2 hours at a steady pace; guests with heart, lung, or circulation conditions should consult their doctor before booking. Tumpak Sewu requires confidence with balance, descending and ascending with ropes and handholds, and wet, slippery ground. Madakaripura involves wading and uneven canyon terrain.

### What should I wear for the Ijen hike?
Wear warm layers for the midnight start (10–15°C at the crater rim), long sleeves and trousers, and hiking shoes with good grip on a dusty or slippery trail. Bring a headlamp as backup — JVTO provides headlamps on Ijen routes but having your own is sensible. Do not wear silver jewellery on the Ijen hike: sulphur gas reacts with silver and the tarnishing may be difficult or impossible to reverse. This applies to rings, bracelets, necklaces, earrings, and silver-coated watches.
