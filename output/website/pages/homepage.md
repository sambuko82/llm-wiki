### Kawah Ijen (Ijen Crater)

Active volcano at 2,386 m. The pre-dawn Blue Fire phenomenon — sulfuric gases igniting at the crater floor — is visible in suitable weather conditions. The summit holds the world's highest-volume acidic crater lake (pH ≈ 0, turquoise). The ascent from Paltuding trailhead is ~3 km, roughly 90 minutes, on a steep rocky trail. Gas masks are provided by JVTO. Blue Fire is a natural phenomenon subject to weather and gas activity.

*Source: [[destinations/kawah-ijen]] · Voice invariant: approved blue-fire language per [[website/brand-voice]]*

---

### Mount Bromo

Active volcano in the Tengger caldera at 2,329 m. The Penanjakan sunrise viewpoint at 2,770 m is one of Southeast Asia's most photographed morning scenes: caldera floor, sea of sand, active smoking crater in a single frame. The 4WD jeep traverse across Pasir Berbisik is part of the experience. JVTO holds BBKSDA national-park operator clearance — guests enter without paperwork friction.

*Source: [[destinations/mount-bromo]] · [[credentials/legal-licenses]] BBKSDA*

---

### Tumpak Sewu

Curtain waterfall approximately 120 m high in Lumajang Regency, East Java. The main viewpoint sits at the canyon rim; an optional canyon descent brings you to the base. One of the few multi-tiered waterfalls in Java with a guided canyon descent option. Served on four Surabaya-origin and two Bali-origin packages.

*Source: [[destinations/tumpak-sewu]] · [[products/packages-overview]]*

---

### Madakaripura Waterfall

The tallest waterfall in Java, set inside a narrow canyon in Probolinggo Regency. The approach involves wading through a stream into an enclosed rock chamber — bring clothes you are prepared to get fully wet. Often combined with Bromo as a single-day extension. Served on six packages from Surabaya and Bali.

*Source: [[destinations/madakaripura]] · [[products/packages-overview]]*
