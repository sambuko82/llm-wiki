# East Java Tour Permits — What You Need for Bromo, Ijen & More

East Java's volcano and waterfall destinations each have their own entry requirements — park tickets, guide fees, health certificates, jeep allocations. Some are straightforward. Some are regulatory. And some are the kind of thing a properly licensed operator handles quietly while you focus on the experience.

This page covers every permit and entry requirement across JVTO's destinations, what they cost, and how to verify that your operator actually holds the right clearances.

---

## Overview: Why Permits Matter

East Java's major destinations are conservation areas, national parks, or regulated natural sites. Entry requirements exist for:

- **Conservation** — protecting volcanic ecosystems and managed areas
- **Safety** — health screening at Ijen, jeep-only access at Bromo
- **Revenue** — entrance fees fund park management and local communities

Most tourists don't think about permits until they're at the gate. A licensed operator pre-handles everything. An unlicensed one leaves you to figure it out on arrival.

## Operator Licensing — What to Look For

Not all East Java tour operators hold the same clearances. When choosing an operator, verify:

| Credential | What It Proves | How to Check |
|---|---|---|
| **NIB** (Nomor Induk Berusaha) | Legal business registration via OSS | Search on oss.go.id |
| **TDUP** (Tanda Daftar Usaha Pariwisata) | Tourism business registration with Dinas Pariwisata | Request copy from operator |
| **KBLI 79121 + 79911** | Registered as both Travel Agency + Tour Operator | Listed on NIB certificate |
| **BBKSDA operator clearance** | Authorized to operate in national parks and conservation areas | Ask operator to show documentation |
| **HPWKI membership** | Ijen specialist guides — BBKSDA-supervised volcanic safety training | Verify association registration number |

## Red Flags — Signs an Operator May Not Be Properly Licensed

Watch for these when evaluating East Java tour operators:

- **Cannot show NIB or TDUP** when asked — legitimate operators publish these openly
- **Surprise costs at the gate** — entrance fees, guide fees, or gas mask rental charged on arrival means they were not included (or the operator is adding markup at the point of least resistance)
- **No BBKSDA clearance mentioned** — operators working inside Ijen or Bromo without clearance are operating in a grey area
- **"Driver only" or unbundled transport** — operators offering car-only services in conservation areas may not hold the permits required for guided access
- **Generic guide assignment** — if your "Ijen guide" doesn't hold HPWKI certification, they haven't completed BBKSDA-supervised safety training (SAR + emergency medical response)
- **No health-screening coordination for Ijen** — if an operator skips this entirely, they are either unaware of or non-compliant with BBKSDA SE.1658/KSA.9/2024
