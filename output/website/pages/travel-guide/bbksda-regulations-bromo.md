# Bromo National Park Rules — Entry, Jeep & BBKSDA Permits

Bromo Tengger Semeru is a national park with specific entry rules. Understanding them helps you plan — and helps you tell a licensed operator from an improvised one.

---

## Bromo Tengger Semeru National Park

Mount Bromo sits inside the **Bromo Tengger Semeru National Park**, managed by BBKSDA Jawa Timur (Balai Besar Konservasi Sumber Daya Alam Jawa Timur). All commercial tour operations within the park boundary require BBKSDA operator clearance.

The park covers the Tengger caldera — a vast volcanic sand plain ("sea of sand") ringed by the caldera wall, with Bromo's active crater, the Penanjakan sunrise viewpoint (2,770 m), and the Hindu temple Pura Luhur Poten on the caldera floor.

## Jeep Requirement

Private vehicles cannot enter the caldera zone. A **4WD jeep is mandatory** for:

- The pre-dawn drive to **Penanjakan viewpoint** (the sunrise spot)
- The **Sea of Sand traverse** across the caldera floor to the crater base
- Return transport after the crater walk

JVTO pre-arranges jeep allocation on all Bromo-relevant packages. No on-the-day haggling with local jeep operators. The jeep cost (IDR 500,000/unit) is included in package prices.

The jeep ride is not just transport — the pre-dawn traverse across the volcanic sand plain in darkness, headlights cutting through fog, is part of the Bromo experience.

## Closure Rules

Volcanic activity can trigger park closure. Two authorities can order closures:

- **PVMBG** (Pusat Vulkanologi dan Mitigasi Bencana Geologi) — Indonesia's national volcanology agency. Alert levels are published on MAGMA Indonesia.
- **BBKSDA Jawa Timur** — the park authority can close access independently based on local conditions.

JVTO follows all official closure announcements without exception. When a closure affects a booked tour, the **Plan-B SOP** activates:

- Guests are briefed in advance
- Alternative viewpoints or replacement destinations are arranged
- No tour day is lost

This is a written operational policy, not a marketing promise.

## Yadnya Kasada Impact

**Yadnya Kasada** is an annual Hindu ceremony held at Pura Luhur Poten on the Bromo caldera floor. The Tengger community makes offerings into the active crater. The date follows the lunar calendar and shifts each year.

What it means for visitors:

- The Bromo area becomes **extremely crowded** during Kasada
- Park access may be restricted or rerouted
- Accommodation near Cemoro Lawang fills up weeks in advance

JVTO activates Plan-B SOP for bookings in the Kasada window — guests are informed during the booking stage, and alternatives are proposed if the primary itinerary would be significantly affected.

## FAQ

**Q: Do I need a health certificate for Bromo?**
No. Unlike Ijen, Bromo does not have a BBKSDA health-certificate requirement. The altitude and absence of direct sulfuric gas exposure at tourist viewing points make it lower-risk.

**Q: Can I drive my own car to the Bromo crater?**
No. Private vehicles are prohibited in the caldera zone. A 4WD jeep is mandatory for Penanjakan viewpoint access and the Sea of Sand traverse. JVTO pre-arranges jeep allocation — included in all Bromo package prices.

**Q: What happens if Bromo is closed on my tour date?**
JVTO follows official closure announcements from PVMBG and BBKSDA. If Bromo closes for volcanic activity, JVTO activates the Plan-B framework — guests are briefed in advance, alternative viewpoints or destinations are arranged, and no tour day is lost.

**Q: Should I avoid booking during Yadnya Kasada?**
Not necessarily, but plan ahead. The ceremony causes heavy crowding. JVTO informs guests during booking if their dates overlap with Kasada and proposes alternatives if the impact would be significant.
