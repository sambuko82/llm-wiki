# What to Pack for Your Bromo & Ijen Volcano Tour

East Java’s volcanic terrain demands careful packing. In a single day, you will experience temperatures near freezing (5°C to 10°C) before dawn at high altitudes, followed by warm daytime sunshine (20°C to 25°C), and wet canyon trails if your tour includes Madakaripura or Tumpak Sewu waterfalls. 

Here is the definitive packing checklist verified by the JVTO operations team.

---

## 1. Clothing Layering System

Do not pack a single heavy winter coat. Instead, pack layers you can easily shed as the sun rises and you generate body heat during the climb.

- **Base Layer**: A moisture-wicking thermal top and bottom (merino wool or synthetic). Avoid cotton, which traps sweat and chills your body.
- **Mid-Layer**: A fleece jacket or lightweight down jacket to trap warm air.
- **Outer Shell**: A windproof and water-resistant jacket. Wind chill on the Penanjakan Bromo sunrise deck and the Ijen crater rim can be severe.
- **Trekking Pants**: Long, lightweight, stretchy pants. Avoid denim jeans, which are heavy and restrict movement on steep trails.
- **Warm Accessories**: A wool beanie to cover your ears, lightweight windproof gloves, and a neck gaiter or scarf.

---

## 2. Footwear: Grip is Critical

Both volcanoes feature loose gravel, sand, and steep slopes. Slipping is the most common cause of minor injuries.

- **Hiking Shoes**: Sturdy trail-running shoes or mid-cut hiking boots with a deep tread (Vibram or rubber lugs). Paved and unpaved trails are slippery when covered in dry volcanic ash or wet mud.
- **Socks**: Merino wool or synthetic hiking socks to prevent blisters. Pack a spare pair.
- **Canyon Sandals**: If your tour includes Madakaripura or Tumpak Sewu, bring strap-on water shoes or adventure sandals. You will be wading through water streams.

---

## 3. Volcano Safety Gear: What JVTO Provides

JVTO coordinates your safety equipment so you don't have to carry heavy gear across Indonesia. The following items are provided at the trailhead:

- **Professional Gas Mask**: Sanitized half-face industrial respirators with chemical cartridges to filter out sulfur dioxide (SO2) gases at Ijen.
- **Trekking Poles**: Adjustable poles to assist with balance on Ijen's steep unpaved trail and the optional crater descent.
- **Headlamp**: LED headlamps for the midnight ascent of Kawah Ijen.

---

## 4. Personal Items & Protection

- **Sunglasses**: The sun reflecting off Ijen's white volcanic ash and sulfur rocks can be blinding.
- **Sunscreen & Lip Balm**: High-altitude sun is intense, and dry volcanic wind quickly cracks lips.
- **Rain Poncho**: A lightweight raincoat is essential. For Madakaripura Waterfall, you will walk through heavy canyon spray and *will* get wet.
- **Personal Medications**: Bring your own prescription medicines. 
  > *Note: A health certificate is mandatory for every guest before Kawah Ijen crater entry (BBKSDA SE.1658/KSA.9/2024). JVTO coordinates the mandatory clinic screening workflow with our medical officer (BBKSDA SE.1658/KSA.9/2024). If you have a history of asthma or heart conditions, consult a physician before booking.*

---

## 5. Travel Logistics checklist

- **Original Passport**: Required at the Kawah Ijen entry checkpoint and hotel check-ins.
- **Cash in IDR**: JVTO tours are all-inclusive, but you will need Indonesian Rupiah (IDR) for tips, personal snacks, and souvenirs. 
- **Travel Voucher**: A digital copy of your JVTO E-Voucher saved on your phone.
