# BBKSDA Ijen Regulations — Health Certificate, Entry & Permits

Kawah Ijen is a conservation area under direct government authority. Understanding the regulations before you book saves confusion at the gate — and helps you spot operators who skip the rules.

---

## What Is BBKSDA?

**BBKSDA** stands for **Balai Besar Konservasi Sumber Daya Alam Jawa Timur** — the East Java office of Indonesia's nature conservation authority. BBKSDA manages Kawah Ijen as a Natural Tourism Park, sets access rules, issues operator clearances, and enforces entry requirements at the Paltuding gate.

Key responsibilities at Ijen:

- Setting park entrance fees and ticketing (via tiket.bbksdajatim.org)
- Issuing and enforcing the health-certificate regulation (SE.1658/KSA.9/2024)
- Authorizing tour operators to work inside the conservation area
- Supervising guide training programs through HPWKI
- Ordering closures based on volcanic gas levels and safety conditions

## Health Certificate (Surat Sehat) — SE.1658/KSA.9/2024

The health certificate requirement is set by **BBKSDA**, not by tour operators. Under Surat Edaran SE.1658/KSA.9/2024, a recent local health certificate is required for every guest before Ijen crater access.

**How it works:**

1. A licensed medical doctor measures vitals: oxygen saturation (SpO₂), blood pressure, heart rate, and respiratory history
2. Results are recorded digitally into the Ijen Digital Health Security System (health.mountijen.com)
3. A QR-verified **surat sehat** is issued if the guest passes
4. BBKSDA staff scan the QR code at the crater access gate

**No valid QR code = no access to the crater zone.** This is a hard gate — not a formality.

JVTO's Ijen health screenings are conducted by Dr. Ahmad Irwandanu, a licensed medical doctor (Dokter Umum) whose STR credential (QN00001073380217) is publicly verifiable through Kemenkes RI (SatuSehat SDMK) and KKI. Screening takes place at the guest's hotel before departure or at a local partner clinic.

If a guest is assessed unfit, the climb is cancelled for that individual — not the group. The screening cost is non-refundable. This is a BBKSDA rule, not JVTO discretion.

## HPWKI Guide Certification

**HPWKI** (Himpunan Pelaku Wisata Khusus Ijen) is the Ijen specialist guide association. Membership requires completing BBKSDA-supervised training — not a self-certification.

The training program is hosted by BBKSDA at Paltuding (the Ijen trailhead) and covers:

- Conservation area awareness
- Local guide responsibilities inside the Natural Tourism Park
- **SAR (Search and Rescue) techniques**
- **PPGD — Emergency Medical Response** (Penanggulangan Penderita Gawat Darurat)

The most recent documented session ran May 20–22, 2024, targeting 250 guides. JVTO guides hold HPWKI membership (AHU-0001072.AH.01.07.TAHUN 2024).

This is not a printable diploma. It is a government-supervised training pipeline in volcanic safety and emergency response — run by the park authority, at the operational site.

## FAQ

**Q: Is the Ijen health screening mandatory?**
Ijen crater access can require a recent local health certificate when BBKSDA SE.1658/KSA.9/2024 thresholds apply. This is a regulatory requirement set by BBKSDA — not a JVTO policy. When screening is required, JVTO coordinates the entire workflow.

**Q: Can I bring my own gas mask to Ijen?**
You can, but JVTO provides gas masks as a standard inclusion on all Ijen packages (IDR 55,000/person value, included in your package price). JVTO-provided masks are industrial-grade and suited to the SO₂ and HCl environment at the crater.

**Q: What happens if BBKSDA closes Ijen on my tour date?**

**Q: How do I verify that my operator has BBKSDA clearance?**
