### Hero Body (~150 words)

Foreign travelers searching for Mount Bromo and Mount Ijen tours in East Java often arrive at the same problem: the headline price quoted online is rarely the price they actually pay. The advertised IDR figure does not include the Bromo Jeep, the BBKSDA entrance fee, the gas mask rental at Ijen, or sometimes the breakfast that was implied in "with accommodation." On the road, the additions arrive one by one — and a tourist who has already flown 12 hours has limited leverage to negotiate.

JVTO operates on a different model. The voucher we send after deposit lists every cost component. The price on the voucher equals the price quoted in WhatsApp, plus zero. This page is a public, item-by-item statement of what that means.

## Section 1 — What "All-Inclusive" Means at JVTO

For every one of our 16 canonical tour packages, the following items are pre-paid by JVTO and never charged on the road:

**Transport**
- Private air-conditioned vehicle (Toyota Avanza MPV for 1–3 guests, Toyota Hiace minibus for 4–9 guests, Hiace plus MPV for 10–11 guests)
- Dedicated driver assigned to your booking — not shared with another group
- Fuel, road tolls, parking, ferry crossings where applicable (e.g., Gilimanuk–Ketapang for Bali-origin packages)

**Crew**
- Driver-guide (1–3 guest tours) or professional driver plus escort guide (4+ guest tours), English-speaking on guide-led routes
- Licensed local site guides at key locations (e.g., HPWKI-credentialed Ijen specialist guides at the trailhead)

**Permits and entrance fees**
- BBKSDA Bromo Tengger Semeru National Park entrance fees
- BBKSDA Kawah Ijen entrance fees
- Tumpak Sewu Waterfall entrance fees
- Madakaripura Waterfall entrance fees
- Papuma Beach entrance fees (on packages that include Papuma)
- Any administrative or vehicle permits required at each gate

**Accommodation**
- Hotel or guesthouse accommodation with breakfast at every overnight phase of your itinerary
- Hotel allocation listed per phase in our packages-itineraries reference

**Route-specific equipment (where the route requires it)**
- Private 4WD Mount Bromo Jeep — approximately 4 guests per jeep, additional jeeps arranged for larger groups
- Gas masks for the Ijen Crater hike
- Trekking poles for Ijen routes

**Ijen-specific safety coordination** (on Ijen-relevant packages, where BBKSDA access rules apply)
- Health-screening workflow coordination with our licensed physician Dr. Ahmad Irwandanu (STR verifiable through Kemenkes SatuSehat SDMK), conducted before the pre-dawn hike when BBKSDA SE.1658/KSA.9/2024 thresholds require it

**Comfort items**
- Daily mineral water supply
- Complimentary JVTO travel T-shirt (1 per participant)
- Full pick-up to drop-off assistance — confirmed pickup point, no third-party handoffs

The complete itinerary, hotel allocation, vehicle assignment, and crew assignment are listed on the voucher you receive after deposit. The voucher is the booking reference for the duration of your trip.

## Section 2 — What Is NOT Included (Stated Upfront)

The same principle that requires us to list inclusions in writing requires us to list exclusions just as clearly. The following items are not included in our package prices:

- International and domestic flights
- Travel insurance (recommended, but at your discretion)
- Tips for drivers and guides (at your discretion; not built into the package)
- Personal expenses — snacks, souvenirs, laundry (recommend allocating at least IDR 500,000 per person across a multi-day trip)
- Meals not stated in the itinerary
- Indonesian visa, if applicable to your nationality
- Helmets at Madakaripura Waterfall — these are provided by the local site management at the canyon entrance, not by JVTO (we tell you this upfront so it is not a surprise on the day)
- Optional add-ons you specifically request that are not in your booked package

If something is on this list, it is not in the price. If something is not on this list and not in your voucher, it is also not in the price. The two lists together are exhaustive.

## Section 3 — The Comparison That Matters

Foreign travelers comparing options in East Java face three real categories of operator: licensed all-inclusive (JVTO and a handful of similar operators), online travel agency (OTA — Klook, Booking.com, GetYourGuide), and unlicensed local driver. The headline prices differ. The fully-loaded prices often do not.

| What you actually pay | JVTO (Licensed All-Inclusive) | OTA (e.g., aggregator listing) | Unlicensed Local Driver |
|---|---|---|---|
| Private vehicle + driver | Included | Often included | Included (but vehicle quality variable) |
| English-speaking guide | Included on guide-led routes | Sometimes | Rarely (driver acts as informal guide) |
| BBKSDA entrance fees (Ijen, Bromo) | Included | Sometimes — read fine print | Usually charged extra on the road |
| Bromo 4WD Jeep | Included on Bromo routes | Often charged separately | Charged separately |
| Ijen gas masks | Included on Ijen routes | Rare | Rented at the trailhead by the guest |
| Hotel with breakfast | Included | Some packages | Rare — guest books separately |
| Ijen health-screening coordination (mandatory for every guest) | Included | Not coordinated by the platform | Not coordinated; guest must arrange |
| Trekking poles | Included on Ijen routes | Rare | Rare |
| Written policy pack (booking, payment, cancellation) | Published | Platform policy applies | None |
| Cancellation recourse | 100% Lifetime Travel Credit if ≥48h before Day 1 | Platform's standard refund window | Discretionary — usually deposit forfeited |
| Licensed Indonesian entity (NIB + TDUP) | Yes — PT Java Volcano Rendezvous, NIB 1102230032918, TDUP issued 2023-02-11 | The platform is licensed; the operator may not be | None |
| Tourist Police chain of accountability | Founder is an active Tourist Police officer (Bripka Agung Sambuko, Ditpamobvit East Java) | Indirect, platform handles disputes | None |

The honest version: OTA listings can sometimes match a licensed operator's inclusions. The variability is the problem. Two listings on the same OTA for "Bromo Ijen 3D2N" can differ by IDR 800,000 because one includes the Jeep and one does not. Reading the fine print on every listing is a real cost that travelers usually do not account for.

JVTO's commitment is to remove that cost. One voucher, one price, one chain of accountability.

## Section 4 — The Underlying Logic (Why We Operate This Way)

JVTO's founder, Bripka Agung Sambuko, is an active officer of the Indonesian Tourist Police (Ditpamobvit, East Java). The operating model of the company — written rules, listed inclusions, published policy pack — reflects that institutional background. We treat the tour as a documented operation. The voucher is the documentation.

This is also the framework that makes our Lifetime Travel Credit policy possible. If you have to cancel at least 48 hours before Day 1, 100% of your eligible booking value converts to JVTO Travel Credit — with no expiry date, transferable, and (within the limits of our policy pack) giftable or sellable to another traveler. We can offer that because the price you booked is the price on the voucher; there are no hidden margins inside the bundle that the company is trying to protect.
