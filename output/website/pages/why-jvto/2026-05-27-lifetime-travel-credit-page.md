## Section 1 — How It Actually Works

**The 48-hour cutoff** (local Indonesia time, GMT+7)

- **≥48 hours before Day 1**: 100% of your eligible booking value converts to JVTO Travel Credit, IDR-denominated.
- **<48 hours before Day 1**: booking value is forfeited up to 100%; Travel Credit and cash refunds are generally not issued. The reason is operational — by 48 hours before departure, JVTO has already pre-committed hotel rooms, crew assignments, vehicle allocations, BBKSDA entrance permits, and (on Ijen routes) screening clinic slots. Those costs do not return to us.

**What the Credit covers**

The Credit equals 100% of the *eligible* booking value — that is the deposit and any balance you have paid, minus only any costs that JVTO has explicitly listed as non-recoverable in the original voucher (extremely rare; flagged in writing upfront).

**Where the Credit can be used**

- Any of the 16 canonical JVTO tour packages — from a 1D1N Bromo Sunrise to a 6D5N Surabaya–Malang multi-destination route
- Any future date, with availability confirmed at booking time
- Combined with another payment if you want to upgrade to a longer or larger package
- Applied to a different group size — if your originally-2-pax booking becomes a 4-pax trip later, the Credit value still applies

**The transfer mechanic**

JVTO Travel Credit is transferable with written confirmation. In practice, this means: you email or WhatsApp us with the name and contact of the person you want to transfer to, we confirm the transfer in writing (typically within one business day), and the named recipient holds the credit going forward. Common transfer scenarios: family member who travels in your place, a friend you gift the credit to, a co-organizer of a postponed group booking.

**One free reschedule path** (separate from cancellation)

If you don't need to cancel but only need to move dates, you have a separate option: one free reschedule of the same package, subject to availability, when requested at least 48 hours before Day 1. This is not a Credit issuance — it is a same-package date change, no fee.

## Section 2 — Why JVTO Operates This Way

Three reasons, all of them operational and honest:

**Reason 1 — The insurance market in Indonesia is thin for foreign-issued cards**

A traveler from Singapore or Germany booking a private East Java tour 6 months in advance has limited insurance options that will cover a cancellation initiated by them. Most foreign travel insurance only covers cancellation triggered by documented illness, death, or government travel ban. Personal reasons — work emergency, relationship change, financial setback — are typically excluded. The Lifetime Travel Credit fills this gap. The money you committed is not lost; it waits for you.

**Reason 2 — Volcanic and weather risk is shared, not transferred**

Mount Ijen and Mount Bromo are active volcanoes. BBKSDA may close access at short notice. PVMBG may raise alert levels. Eruptive activity, gas, fog, severe weather — these are real risks built into the geography. We absorb operational risk on our side (force majeure clause kicks in — see Section 5). The Lifetime Travel Credit is the corresponding fairness for your side: if you are not the one who triggered the cancellation, you are not the one who pays for it.

**Reason 3 — The model only works if we are honest about deposit commitments**

A deposit is not abstract money sitting in our account. By the time you are 14 days out from Day 1, that deposit has paid a BBKSDA entrance commitment, a hotel block, and a crew schedule. Issuing cash refunds at 100% would force us to fold those commitments back — at our cost — every time a traveler reconsidered. The Credit model lets us honor the full value of your commitment while keeping the supplier chain intact. Both sides preserved.

## Section 3 — Three Scenarios Where the Policy Worked

These are illustrative composite scenarios reflecting common situations the policy is designed for. Specific guest details are anonymized.

**Scenario 1 — Family emergency, 4 months out**

A guest from the UK booked a 4D3N Ijen-Papuma-Tumpak Sewu-Bromo tour 8 months ahead for a March arrival. Three months before the trip, a family member's medical emergency required them to stay home. They messaged JVTO five days before the original Day 1 — well within the 48-hour cutoff. Outcome: 100% of the IDR 9,800,000 deposit + balance paid converted to Lifetime Travel Credit. The guest rebooked the same package for 18 months later, paid the seasonal price difference, and traveled.

**Scenario 2 — Group changes from 8 to 6**

A booking organizer in Singapore booked a 5D4N package for 8 guests. Two guests dropped out 6 weeks before departure for personal reasons. The organizer requested Credit transfer for the two affected portions to specific named individuals (one a family member, one a friend). JVTO confirmed both transfers in writing within one business day. The friend used their credit 11 months later for a 3D2N package. The family member is still holding theirs.

**Scenario 3 — Flight disruption from origin country**

A couple from Europe had their connecting flight cancelled by their airline due to weather at a hub airport. They contacted JVTO 36 hours before Day 1 — inside the 48-hour cutoff. In this specific situation, JVTO's force majeure clause (Section 5) applied because the original flight disruption was outside the guest's control. JVTO offered a partial accommodation: rescheduled the tour by 5 days (within hotel and crew availability) and absorbed the hotel rate difference. This is not the standard outcome inside 48 hours — it depended on the documentation and the supplier flexibility we happened to have.

## Section 4 — What Tour Operators and OTAs Typically Do

| Cancellation handling | JVTO | Klook / GetYourGuide (OTA) | Average local unlicensed operator |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cancel ≥48h before | 100% as Lifetime Travel Credit, no expiry, transferable | Platform-defined refund window — typically 24h–7 days; cash refund minus platform fees | Discretionary; deposit usually forfeited |
| Cancel <48h before | Booking forfeited | Usually 0% refund | Forfeited |
| Force majeure / volcano closure | Alternative offered; Credit for unused portion; partial cash refund possible net of non-recoverable expenses | Platform handles dispute; outcome variable | None — operator may simply not show |
| Reschedule | One free reschedule ≥48h, same package, availability-bound | Platform-dependent; rebooking fees common | Discretionary |
| Credit transferability | Yes — to named recipient with written confirmation | Rare; credit usually account-bound | None |
| Credit expiry | None | Typically 6–12 months | None (because no credit issued) |

The OTA comparison is the most important one for foreign travelers. OTAs handle dispute resolution at the platform level — not the operator level — which means you are negotiating with the platform's customer-service department, not the operator who is actually running your trip. JVTO is a direct booking. The cancellation conversation is between you and us, in writing.

## Section 5 — The Honest Fine Print

This section is the part most policy pages avoid. Read it before you book.

**Force majeure (separate path from voluntary cancellation)**

If JVTO cancels, postpones, or reroutes the trip due to volcanic activity, gas activity, earthquakes, floods, government closures, civil unrest, or severe weather, we may offer: reasonable alternative routing, Travel Credit for the unused portion, or partial/full cash refund net of non-recoverable expenses. The specific outcome depends on what supplier costs we have already committed at the time of cancellation. We document this transparently when it occurs.

**Health screening denial (Ijen routes)**

If a guest is denied medical clearance on an Ijen-relevant package, the affected itinerary components are non-refundable. The reason: by the day of screening, BBKSDA entrance permits, hotel rooms, and crew assignments have all been committed. We can only avoid this by encouraging early honest disclosure during booking — if you have a known cardiovascular or respiratory condition, tell us at the inquiry stage so we can propose an Ijen-free route from the same departure city.

**Force majeure on the guest side does not automatically waive the 48-hour rule**

A guest's own medical emergency, family emergency, or flight disruption inside 48 hours is handled case-by-case. The 48-hour rule is the default. Genuine documented emergencies are reviewed against operational reality (what we can recover from suppliers) — but we cannot promise a default exemption. Inside 48 hours, the financial commitment is largely irreversible from our side.

A: No. JVTO Travel Credit is non-expiring. You can use it for any of our 16 canonical tour packages, on any future date, in any future year.

**Q: Can I transfer JVTO Travel Credit to another person?**
A: Yes. JVTO Travel Credit is transferable with written confirmation from JVTO. Email or WhatsApp us with the name and contact of the recipient; we confirm the transfer in writing, typically within one business day.

**Q: What is the cancellation cutoff time for getting Travel Credit?**
A: 48 hours before Day 1, local Indonesia time (GMT+7). Cancellations at least 48 hours before Day 1 receive 100% Lifetime Travel Credit. Cancellations within 48 hours generally result in booking forfeiture.
