# Our Team

14 field crew members — 7 guides and 7 drivers. Every guide and driver is individually named, photographed, and license-linked. Five guides hold HPWKI KTA credentials (Kartu Tanda Anggota) issued after BBKSDA-supervised volcanic safety training covering SAR, First Aid, and Ijen crater protocols. KTA registration covers 11 members; 3 additional drivers (Yusuf, Dika, Pras) are confirmed active in our system with KTA codes pending verification.

This is documented in the BBKSDA Jawa Timur press record of the HPWKI Pelatihan Pemandu Kawah Ijen training programme (2024-05-24, https://bbksdajatim.org/pelatihan-pemandu-kawah-ijen/) — an independent institutional record that the training exists and that HPWKI guides completed it.

No anonymous freelancers. No marketplace crew.

## Mr. Sam — Founder

**Agung "Mr. Sam" Sambuko**
Founder, Operational Lead, Active Tourist Police Officer

Mr. Sam is an active officer in Ditpamobvit — the Indonesian National Police directorate for security at vital tourist objects, including the Ijen Crater area. He is the operational authority behind every JVTO route decision and safety call. His police status is independently documented in three press articles (Detik.com 2021-03-14, Radar Jember 2021-03-24, Radar Jember 2021-05-27) and in SPRIN police documents on file.

He founded JVTO from a guesthouse in Bondowoso in 2015, incorporated PT Java Volcano Rendezvous in 2016, and built the private-only model in direct response to safety gaps he observed in the field as a Tourist Police officer.

No other East Java volcano operator is led by an active Tourist Police officer. This is not a marketing claim — it is a police assignment verifiable through institutional records.

## HPWKI KTA Chain — What the Credential Proves

A guide holding a JVTO KTA card is a registered member of HPWKI (AHU-0001072.AH.01.07.TAHUN 2024) — a state-recognized association for Ijen crater operators. HPWKI membership requires completing volcanic safety training supervised by BBKSDA Jawa Timur, covering SAR protocols and First Aid certification.

The training programme is documented independently by BBKSDA in their own institutional press record (2024-05-24). The chain runs: guest sees crew → crew holds KTA → KTA issued by HPWKI → HPWKI verified at AHU → HPWKI training documented by BBKSDA.

All 5 KTA-confirmed guides (Anjas, Taufik, Rendi, Kiki, Gufron) are confirmed HPWKI members through this chain.
