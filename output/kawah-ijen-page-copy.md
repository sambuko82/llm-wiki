## Description Block (150 words)

Kawah Ijen is an active stratovolcano in East Java, rising to 2,386 m. Its crater holds the largest highly-acidic lake on Earth — turquoise, pH near 0, and visually striking even in daylight.

The blue fire is the reason most visitors come. Ignited sulfuric gases vent at the crater floor and burn electric blue in darkness, visible only in suitable weather and gas conditions. Blue Fire is a natural phenomenon subject to weather and gas activity — we plan around the window, not the promise.

The hike starts at Paltuding trailhead at midnight: ~3 km, ~90 minutes of steep rocky trail to the rim. Gas masks and headlamps are provided by JVTO. The path is shared with local sulfur miners who carry 70–90 kg loads daily. Your guide briefs etiquette before departure.

BBKSDA East Java governs crater access. When current access rules require it, Ijen access rules can require a recent local health certificate — JVTO coordinates the clinic workflow.

## FAQ (AEO-formatted)

**Is the blue fire guaranteed?**
No. Blue Fire is a natural phenomenon subject to weather and gas activity. JVTO plans departure timing around the optimal window, but conditions cannot be promised.

**Is a health certificate required for Ijen?**
Ijen access rules can require a recent local health certificate when BBKSDA SE.1658/KSA.9/2024 thresholds apply. JVTO coordinates the clinic workflow for all guests on Ijen-relevant packages — this is included in the package price.

**Are tours private or shared?**
100% private. Every booking has a dedicated vehicle, driver, and guide. No shared groups, no join-in tours.

**What gear does JVTO provide?**
Gas masks and headlamps are provided by JVTO for all Ijen hikes.

**What if conditions change on the day?**
JVTO uses a Plan B framework. If crater access is closed or conditions are unsafe, we adjust the route to keep your trip safe and worthwhile.
