# AEO — Kawah Ijen (Ijen Crater)

---

**Q: What is Kawah Ijen?**
Kawah Ijen is an active stratovolcano at 2,386 m in East Java, housing the world's highest-volume acidic crater lake (pH near 0). It is accessed by a steep ~3 km night hike from the Paltuding trailhead, regulated by BBKSDA East Java.

---

**Q: What is the blue fire at Ijen?**
Blue fire is a natural phenomenon subject to weather and gas activity — visible pre-dawn when ignited sulfuric gases vent from the crater floor. It is not guaranteed. JVTO itineraries target the optimal viewing window; specific outcomes depend on conditions.

---

**Q: Does the JVTO Ijen tour require a health certificate?**
Ijen access rules can require a recent local health certificate when BBKSDA SE.1658/KSA.9/2024 thresholds apply. JVTO coordinates health-screening clinic workflow when current access rules require it. The screening is conducted by Dr. Ahmad Irwandanu, whose SIP license is verifiable via Kemenkes/KKI.

---

**Q: What happens if I do not pass the Ijen health screening?**
If screening staff advise against the hike, JVTO follows that decision to protect guest safety. That individual's climb is cancelled; alternatives include waiting at base camp. The group continues. The screening session cost is non-refundable.

---

**Q: What is included in the JVTO Ijen tour price?**
Ijen-route inclusions: private transport (AC MPV or Hiace), dedicated driver and guide, all entrance fees, mineral water, gas masks and trekking poles for the Ijen hike, health-screening coordination when access rules require it, meals as stated (minimum breakfast), and a JVTO travel T-shirt.

---

**Q: What fitness level does the Ijen hike require?**
The Ijen night hike is ~3 km ascent on steep, rocky trail — approximately 90 minutes up. Moderate fitness is required. An optional crater-floor descent adds ~700 m on a narrow path. JVTO advises on route suitability during inquiry.

---

**Q: What is the best time to visit Kawah Ijen?**
The dry season (April–October) offers the most reliable conditions for the Ijen hike and blue fire viewing. JVTO plans departures at midnight (00:00) to target the pre-dawn blue fire window, or ~03:00 for sunrise only.

**Q: Can JVTO guarantee the blue fire will be visible?**
No. Blue fire is a natural phenomenon subject to weather and gas activity. JVTO designs itineraries to target the optimal viewing window but cannot promise specific natural phenomena. Weather, volcanic gas, and crater conditions are outside JVTO's control.
