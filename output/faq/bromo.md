## A. About Mount Bromo

### What is Mount Bromo?

Mount Bromo is an active stratovolcano at 2,329 m, sitting inside the vast Tengger caldera in Probolinggo Regency, East Java, Indonesia. It is best known for the Penanjakan sunrise viewpoint (2,770 m), the "Sea of Sand" caldera floor traversed by 4WD jeep, and the active smoking crater. Bromo is protected within Bromo Tengger Semeru National Park, administered by BBKSDA East Java. It is one of the most visited volcanic destinations in Southeast Asia.

### What is Mount Bromo's current volcanic activity status?

As of 2026-05-16, PVMBG (Indonesian Volcanology Agency) elevated Mount Bromo to Level II Waspada, with a mandatory 1 km exclusion zone measured from the crater rim. This means the crater stairs, crater rim approach, and the area immediately surrounding the active vent are currently off-limits to visitors. The Penanjakan sunrise viewpoint, the Sea of Sand caldera floor, and all jeep routes are outside the exclusion zone and remain fully accessible. JVTO monitors official PVMBG status continuously and confirms the current itinerary with guests before arrival.

### Is it still safe to visit Mount Bromo at Level II Waspada?

Yes, for the key visitor experiences. The 1 km exclusion zone targets the crater rim approach — not the broader caldera. Penanjakan viewpoint (2,770 m, reached by jeep on a separate access road) and the Sea of Sand traverse are well outside the restricted area and remain open. JVTO holds BBKSDA national-park operator authorisation and follows all official access instructions. The Bromo sunrise and caldera jeep experience are available and safe under current Level II conditions. JVTO will not take guests to the crater rim while Level II is in effect. Licensed operator NIB 1102230032918.

### When is the best time to visit Mount Bromo?

The dry season — April through October — offers clearer skies and more reliable sunrise conditions at Penanjakan. Visiting during the wet season (November–March) is possible but cloud cover is more frequent and trails may be muddy. JVTO monitors conditions and activates Plan B alternatives when access is restricted; guests are briefed before their departure day.

### Does visiting Bromo require a permit?

Yes. Bromo Tengger Semeru National Park requires a BBKSDA-issued operator permit. JVTO holds the relevant national-park operator authorisation (see [[credentials/legal-licenses]]). Guests do not handle their own paperwork — permits are pre-arranged as part of every Bromo-inclusive package.

## B. The Sunrise & Penanjakan Viewpoint

### What is the Penanjakan viewpoint?

Penanjakan is the primary Bromo sunrise viewpoint at 2,770 m — higher than Bromo itself. From here, guests look down across the full Tengger caldera: the Sea of Sand, the active smoking crater cone, and the surrounding caldera walls. It is one of the most photographed sunrises in Southeast Asia. JVTO 4WD jeeps reach Penanjakan via a dedicated access road. Penanjakan is outside the current Level II exclusion zone and is fully accessible.

### What time does the JVTO tour depart for the Bromo sunrise?

Departure for Penanjakan is typically between 01:00 and 02:00 WIB to arrive at the viewpoint before first light. On overnight packages, JVTO departs the hotel at approximately 02:00, combining Milky Way stargazing and the sunrise sequence. On the 1D1N Bromo Midnight package, guests depart Surabaya after midnight, arriving at the Bromo side for the pre-dawn window without an overnight stay.

### Is the Bromo sunrise guaranteed?

No. Cloud cover, rain, and mist at Penanjakan are weather-dependent and cannot be guaranteed. JVTO designs itineraries to maximise the chance of clear conditions by targeting the optimal departure window, but natural outcomes are outside the operator's control. JVTO does not offer refunds or Travel Credit when weather affects sunrise visibility.

## C. The Jeep & Caldera Floor

### Is the 4WD jeep included in the price?

Yes. A private 4WD jeep for the Bromo crater area is a standard inclusion on all Bromo-inclusive JVTO packages. The jeep covers the caldera floor sequence: Penanjakan viewpoint, Sea of Sand traverse, and crater drop-off point. JVTO pre-arranges the jeep — guests do not haggle for a vehicle on arrival day. Licensed operator NIB 1102230032918.

### How many guests fit in one Bromo jeep?

Each jeep accommodates a maximum of approximately 4 guests for comfort and safety. Groups of more than 4 have additional jeeps arranged. Jeep allocation is confirmed during booking based on group size. The cost of additional jeeps is included in the package pricing — no per-jeep surcharge is added on the day.

### What is the Sea of Sand at Bromo?

The Sea of Sand (Pasir Berbisik) is the caldera floor surrounding the Bromo crater cone — approximately 10 km² of volcanic sand contained within the Tengger caldera walls. All JVTO jeep routes cross the Sea of Sand between Penanjakan and the crater drop-off area. The traverse is part of the experience, not a transit segment. The Sea of Sand is fully accessible under the current Level II restrictions.

### Can I approach the Bromo crater rim?

Under the current Level II Waspada status (in effect from 2026-05-16), a 1 km exclusion zone around the crater rim is mandated by PVMBG. JVTO does not take guests to the crater stairs or rim while Level II is active. The jeep drop-off area on the caldera floor, the Sea of Sand, and the Penanjakan viewpoint all remain fully accessible. JVTO will confirm the applicable itinerary before your arrival. When Bromo returns to Level I, the short walk to the crater rim (via paved steps from the jeep drop-off) is included as part of the standard jeep sequence.

### Is Milky Way stargazing available at Bromo?

On overnight packages with a 02:00 WIB departure, guests can observe the Milky Way from the Penanjakan area before sunrise — weather permitting. The pre-dawn darkness at 2,770 m, away from urban light pollution, offers good conditions when skies are clear. This is part of the pre-dawn waiting time at the viewpoint, not a separate programme item. Cloud cover or rain will prevent visibility.

## E. Fitness & What to Bring

### How physically demanding is a Bromo visit?

The Bromo visit is accessible to most fitness levels. Penanjakan is reached by 4WD jeep — no hiking required for the sunrise viewpoint. Under normal Level I conditions, the walk from the jeep drop-off to the crater stairs is approximately 1.5 km on flat volcanic sand, with a short paved ascent to the rim. Under the current Level II restriction, the crater approach is not accessible, so physical demand is limited to the flat caldera floor area. No health screening is required for Bromo-only packages.

### What should I wear at Mount Bromo?

Temperatures at the Penanjakan viewpoint and caldera floor are 5–15°C (41–59°F) in the pre-dawn window. Layering is essential: a warm base layer, a fleece or down mid-layer, and a windproof outer. Gloves and a warm hat are advisable. Closed shoes are sufficient — the caldera floor is volcanic sand and (under normal conditions) the crater approach uses paved steps. A dust mask or light scarf helps at and near the crater zone due to volcanic emissions.

### Do I need to carry my main luggage for the jeep sequence?

No. Main luggage stays at the hotel on overnight packages; guests carry only a small daypack for the jeep and sunrise sequence. On the 1D1N Midnight package (no hotel stay), luggage remains in the vehicle during the jeep sequence. JVTO confirms logistics in the pre-trip guide issued with your e-voucher.

## F. Closures, Festivals & Plan B

### What happens if Mount Bromo closes for volcanic activity?

JVTO follows official PVMBG, BBKSDA, and National Park management instructions. If Bromo's crater zone or approach is restricted — as under the current Level II Waspada (from 2026-05-16) — JVTO activates its Plan-B framework: adjusted itinerary focusing on accessible areas (Penanjakan, Sea of Sand), alternative viewpoints, or rescheduled segments. Guests are never left without a programme. The Plan-B framework is a written policy, not a verbal promise — see [[products/packages-overview]] §closure-policy.

### What is the Yadnya Kasada festival and does it affect Bromo access?

Yadnya Kasada is the annual Tengger Hindu ceremony held at Pura Luhur Poten on the Bromo caldera floor. The date varies each year on the Tengger lunar calendar. During this period, the caldera access road and crater stairs may be restricted or heavily congested. JVTO activates a Plan-B SOP for bookings falling during Kasada — guests are informed in advance and receive an alternative programme. JVTO does not book guests onto dates where access is impossible without briefing them first.

### Can rain cancel a Bromo tour?

Light to moderate rain rarely cancels the tour outright, but it reduces sunrise and viewpoint visibility at Penanjakan. The Sea of Sand and caldera floor remain accessible in light rain. JVTO guides assess conditions on the day. Sunrise and viewpoint outcomes are weather-dependent and are not compensable when natural conditions are the cause.
