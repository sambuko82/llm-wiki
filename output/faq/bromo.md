# Mount Bromo — FAQ

The Penanjakan sunrise viewpoint (~5 km from the crater) is **not affected**. ## A. About Mount Bromo

### What is Mount Bromo?

Mount Bromo is an active stratovolcano at 2,329 m, sitting inside the vast Tengger caldera in East Java, Indonesia. It is best known for the Penanjakan sunrise viewpoint, the "Sea of Sand" caldera floor traversed by 4WD jeep, and the active smoking crater reached on foot or by horse from the jeep drop-off. Bromo is protected within Bromo Tengger Semeru National Park, administered by BBKSDA East Java.

### Where is Mount Bromo located?

Bromo lies within Probolinggo Regency, East Java, inside the Tengger caldera — a collapsed volcanic structure shared with several other peaks. The main access gateway is Cemoro Lawang village, approximately 3 hours by road from Surabaya. For Bali visitors, the journey by road and ferry takes 5–7 hours to the Bromo side. JVTO handles all logistics including permits and jeep pre-arrangement.

### When is the best time to visit Mount Bromo?

The dry season — April through October — offers clearer skies and more reliable sunrise conditions at Penanjakan. Visiting in the wet season (November–March) is possible but cloud cover at the viewpoint is more frequent and trails may be muddy. JVTO monitors conditions and activates Plan B alternatives if Bromo closes; guests are briefed before departure day.

### Does Mount Bromo require a permit?

Yes. Bromo Tengger Semeru National Park requires a BBKSDA-issued permit for all operators. JVTO holds the relevant national-park operator authorisation under [[credentials/legal-licenses]]. Guests enter the national park without handling their own paperwork — permits are pre-arranged as part of the package.

## B. The Sunrise Experience

### What is the Penanjakan viewpoint?

Penanjakan is the primary sunrise viewpoint for Mount Bromo, at 2,770 m — higher than Bromo itself. From here, guests look down across the full Tengger caldera: the Sea of Sand, the active smoking crater cone, and the surrounding caldera walls. It is one of the most photographed sunrises in Southeast Asia. JVTO 4WD jeeps reach Penanjakan via a dedicated access road, not by foot trail.

### What time does the JVTO tour depart for the Bromo sunrise?

Departure for Penanjakan is typically between 01:00 and 02:00 WIB to arrive at the viewpoint before first light. On overnight packages, JVTO departs the hotel at approximately 02:00 for a combined Milky Way and sunrise experience. On the 1D1N Bromo Midnight package, guests depart Surabaya after midnight, arriving at the Bromo side for the pre-dawn window without an overnight stay.

### Is the Bromo sunrise guaranteed?

No. Cloud cover, rain, and mist at Penanjakan are weather-dependent and cannot be guaranteed. JVTO designs itineraries to maximise the chance of clear conditions by targeting the optimal departure window, but natural outcomes are not within the operator's control. JVTO does not offer refunds or credits when weather affects sunrise visibility.

## C. The Jeep & Caldera Floor

### Is the 4WD jeep included in the price?

Yes. A private 4WD jeep for the Bromo crater area is a standard inclusion on all Bromo-inclusive JVTO packages. The jeep covers the caldera floor sequence: Penanjakan viewpoint, Sea of Sand traverse, and crater drop-off point. JVTO pre-arranges the jeep — guests do not haggle for a vehicle on the day. Licensed operator NIB 1102230032918.

### How many guests fit in one Bromo jeep?

Each jeep accommodates a maximum of approximately 4 guests for comfort and safety. Groups larger than 4 guests will have additional jeeps arranged. Jeep allocation is confirmed during booking based on group size. The cost of additional jeeps is included in the package pricing — no per-jeep surcharge is added on the day.

### What is the Sea of Sand at Bromo?

The Sea of Sand (Pasir Berbisik) is the caldera floor surrounding the Bromo crater cone — approximately 10 km² of volcanic sand contained within the Tengger caldera walls. All JVTO jeep routes cross the Sea of Sand between Penanjakan and the crater drop-off point. The traverse is part of the experience, not a transit segment.

### Can I ride a horse to the Bromo crater rim?

Under normal conditions (Level I), horse riding from the jeep drop-off to the crater stairs is available on-site — an optional, locally arranged service paid directly to horse handlers, not priced into JVTO packages. Guests who prefer to walk the short flat distance can do so on foot.

**Current Level II restriction**: The crater stairs and rim approach fall within the 1 km exclusion zone active from 2026-05-16. JVTO will not take guests to the crater lip while Level II is in effect. The jeep drop-off area and caldera floor remain accessible — the restriction targets the final ascent to the rim. JVTO confirms the applicable itinerary before your arrival.

### What is at the Bromo crater?

The Bromo crater is an active volcanic vent with a visible smoke plume. Under normal conditions (Level I), a short staircase leads from the jeep drop-off to the crater rim, where guests look directly into the smoking caldera. The temple at the caldera floor — Pura Luhur Poten — is a Tengger Hindu site passed during the jeep sequence. JVTO guides brief guests on respectful conduct at the temple.

**Current Level II restriction (from 2026-05-16)**: The crater rim staircase falls within the PVMBG-mandated 1 km exclusion zone. JVTO does not take guests to the crater lip while Level II Waspada is active. The caldera floor, Sea of Sand, and Penanjakan viewpoint are all accessible. JVTO will confirm the current status and adjusted itinerary before your arrival.

## D. Milky Way & Stargazing

### Is Milky Way stargazing available at Bromo?

On overnight packages with a 02:00 WIB departure, guests can observe the Milky Way from the Penanjakan area before sunrise — weather permitting. The pre-dawn darkness at 2,770 m away from urban light pollution offers good conditions when skies are clear. This is not a separate programme item; it is part of the pre-dawn waiting time at the viewpoint. Cloud cover or rain will prevent visibility. JVTO does not operate dedicated stargazing packages.

## F. Fitness & What to Bring

### How physically demanding is a Bromo visit?

The Bromo visit is accessible to most fitness levels. Penanjakan is reached by 4WD jeep — no hiking required for the viewpoint. The walk from the jeep drop-off to the Bromo crater stairs is approximately 1.5 km on flat volcanic sand, with a short ascent on paved steps to the crater rim. Guests who prefer not to walk can use the optional horse service. No health screening is required for Bromo-only packages.

### What should I wear at Mount Bromo?

Temperatures at the Penanjakan viewpoint and caldera floor are 5–15°C in the pre-dawn window. Layering is essential: a warm base layer, a fleece or down mid-layer, and a windproof outer. Gloves and a hat are advisable. Closed shoes are sufficient — the caldera floor is sand and the steps to the crater are paved. A dust mask or light scarf is useful at the crater rim due to volcanic sulphur emissions.

### Do I need to carry my main luggage for the jeep sequence?

No. Main luggage stays at the hotel on overnight packages; guests carry only a small daypack for the jeep and sunrise sequence. On the 1D1N Midnight package (no hotel stay), luggage remains in the vehicle during the jeep sequence. JVTO confirms logistics in the pre-trip guide issued with your e-voucher.

## G. Closures, Festivals & Plan B

### What is the Yadnya Kasada festival and does it affect Bromo access?

Yadnya Kasada is the annual Tengger Hindu ceremony held at Pura Luhur Poten on the Bromo caldera floor. The date varies each year on the Tengger lunar calendar. During this period, the caldera access road and crater stairs may be restricted or heavily congested. JVTO activates a Plan-B SOP for bookings that fall during Kasada — guests are informed of the adjustment and receive an alternative programme that keeps the trip valuable. JVTO does not book guests onto dates where access is impossible without briefing them in advance.

### What happens if Mount Bromo closes for volcanic activity?

JVTO follows official PVMBG, BBKSDA, and National Park management instructions on closures. If Bromo's crater zone or approach is restricted, JVTO adjusts to available alternative viewpoints (the caldera can often still be entered for sunrise even when the crater stairs are closed), reschedules affected segments, or replaces them with a confirmed safe alternative. Guests are never left without a programme.

**Current status**: Bromo is at Level II Waspada from 2026-05-16. The 1 km crater exclusion zone is in effect. Penanjakan sunrise and the Sea of Sand are unaffected. JVTO has activated Plan-B for the crater rim segment. If you have a confirmed booking that includes the crater approach, JVTO will contact you before your arrival to confirm the adjusted itinerary.

The Plan-B framework is a written policy, not a verbal promise — see [[products/packages-overview]] §closure-policy and [[sources/ssot-v6]] §7_3 weather closures.

### Can rain cancel a Bromo tour?

Light to moderate rain rarely cancels the tour outright, but it reduces sunrise and viewpoint visibility. The Sea of Sand and crater walk remain accessible in light rain. JVTO guides assess conditions on the day. Sunrise and viewpoint outcomes are weather-dependent and are not compensable when natural conditions are the cause.
