## BBKSDA Mount Bromo Regulations FAQ

### What are the BBKSDA entry regulations for Mount Bromo?

Balai Besar Konservasi Sumber Daya Alam (BBKSDA) and Bromo Tengger Semeru National Park (TNBTS) authority require all visitors to book entrance permits online in advance. The national park enforces strict vehicle restrictions: private passenger cars are prohibited from entering the Sea of Sand (Pasir Berbisik) caldera floor. ### What is the safety status of Mount Bromo active crater?

Mount Bromo is an active volcano and is currently at Level II (Waspada) status. BBKSDA regulations strictly prohibit visitors from approaching within a 1 km radius of the active crater floor during heightened activity. JVTO guides monitor alerts from the PVMBG (Volcanology and Geological Hazard Mitigation Center) daily and enforce safety boundaries to protect travelers. ### Why does JVTO use private 4WD jeeps at Mount Bromo?

Under local national park regulations and Tengger community agreements, only registered local 4WD jeeps are permitted to enter the Sea of Sand and navigate the steep climb to the Penanjakan sunrise viewpoints. JVTO includes a private 4WD jeep (limited to a comfortable maximum of 4 guests per jeep) in all Bromo tour packages. We do not use shared jeeps, ensuring you have complete control over your schedule. ### What are the sunrise viewing regulations at Mount Bromo?

To watch the sunrise, jeeps depart from Bromo-side hotels or staging villages (like Cemoro Lawang or Sukapura) around 03:00 WIB to secure parking spots at Penanjakan 1, King Kong Hill, or Seruni Point before dawn. BBKSDA manages designated viewing decks to prevent overcrowding and ensure structural safety. JVTO guides guide you to the best-available viewing spots based on daily crowd traffic. ### Is travel insurance required for visiting Mount Bromo?

While BBKSDA entrance tickets include a basic national park accident insurance coverage, it has very low limits. JVTO and BBKSDA strongly recommend that all international visitors carry comprehensive travel insurance that explicitly covers volcanic adventure tourism and emergency evacuation.
