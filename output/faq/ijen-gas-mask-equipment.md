## Kawah Ijen Gas Mask & Safety Equipment FAQ

### Are gas masks provided by JVTO for the Kawah Ijen hike?

Yes. JVTO provides professional gas masks (respirators) and trekking poles for all Kawah Ijen-inclusive tours at no additional charge. These are standard inclusions in our private packages. ### Why do I need a gas mask at Kawah Ijen?

A gas mask is necessary when descending to the Kawah Ijen crater floor to view the Blue Fire or the acidic lake, where concentrations of sulfur dioxide (SO2) gas can spike. The respirator protects your lungs from acidic fumes that cause breathing difficulty and eye irritation. JVTO guides carry extra safety gear and monitor gas directions constantly. ### What type of gas masks does JVTO provide?

JVTO provides standard industrial half-face respirators equipped with specialized chemical cartridges (activated carbon filters) designed to filter out acidic gases like sulfur dioxide. The masks have adjustable elastic straps to ensure a tight seal around your nose and mouth. Headlamps are also provided for the pre-dawn hike. ### Where do I receive the gas mask and safety gear?

Your dedicated English-speaking JVTO guide will distribute the gas masks, trekking poles, and headlamps at the Paltuding trailhead before the hike begins at 02:00 WIB. The guide will adjust the fit of the mask for each traveler to ensure a proper safety seal. Guests carry the masks on their shoulder straps until reaching the gas-exposed crater zone. ### Can I descent into the Ijen crater without a gas mask?

No, descent into the active volcanic crater zone is forbidden without a functional gas mask due to the extreme toxicity of the volcanic sulfur fumes. BBKSDA rangers and HPWKI-certified guides enforce this rule at the crater rim checkpoint. JVTO guides will instruct you on when to put on the mask as you approach the gas zone.
