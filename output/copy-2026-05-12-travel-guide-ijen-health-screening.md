# Ijen Health Screening — Real Checks, Digital Proof

---

## What the Access Rule Is

BBKSDA Jawa Timur — the government conservation agency responsible for the Kawah Ijen Natural Tourism Park — issued Surat Edaran SE.1658/KSA.9/2024 establishing health-certificate requirements for Ijen crater zone access.

**Ijen access rules can require a recent local health certificate.** JVTO coordinates the clinic workflow when access rules require it.

The certificate requirement is regulatory, set by BBKSDA East Java. It is not unilaterally imposed by JVTO. Whether and when it applies depends on current BBKSDA directives, which JVTO monitors. At the crater access gate, checkpoint staff verify the QR code on the certificate. No valid QR code means no crater zone access.

---

## What the Screening Checks

The health screening is conducted by licensed medical staff. Vitals measured include:

- SpO2 (blood oxygen saturation)
- Blood pressure
- Heart rate
- Respiratory history

Results are recorded digitally and generate a QR code. The QR code allows BBKSDA checkpoint staff at the crater gate to verify that a real screening was completed by a licensed provider — not a paper copy that could be forged.

The QR system is available beyond JVTO guests: participating clinics and hotels in the Ijen area can provide the same screening to non-JVTO travellers. This is not a competitive restriction — it raises the safety standard for all visitors.

---

## The Process — How JVTO Coordinates It

When access rules require health-certificate screening, JVTO arranges the following sequence:

1. **The nurse visits your hotel** the evening before the Ijen hike — in the Bondowoso or Banyuwangi area.
2. **Vitals are checked** at your accommodation. No travel to a clinic is required.
3. **The digital certificate with QR code is issued** for verified guests.
4. **At the Paltuding trailhead**, BBKSDA checkpoint staff scan the QR code before permitting crater zone access.

The entire session takes place at your hotel before departure, not on the trail.

---

## The Doctor — Verifiable Credentials

Health-screening coordination at JVTO uses licensed medical staff. The supervising physician is **Dr. Ahmad Irwandanu**, holding a valid SIP (Surat Izin Praktik) issued by the Indonesian Ministry of Health (Kemenkes RI).

Dr. Irwandanu's SIP is publicly verifiable at: [satusehat.kemkes.go.id/sdmk](https://satusehat.kemkes.go.id/sdmk)

Screening is performed by a credentialed provider under an active government-issued licence — not a formality or a self-certification by JVTO.

---

## What Happens If You Are Not Cleared to Hike

If screening staff or the JVTO team advise that you should not attempt the Ijen hike, that decision is followed. The climb is cancelled for that individual, not the group. Alternatives include waiting at base camp or a safe viewpoint area at Paltuding. The screening session cost is non-refundable. The rest of the group continues.

This outcome protects the individual guest and other guests on the trail. The screening is not a barrier to tourism — it is a check that prevents sending a guest with an undetected condition into a demanding environment with sulphur gas, steep terrain, and limited evacuation options.

---

## Screening Does Not Guarantee a Safe Hike

Health-certificate screening is a pre-hike assessment, not a guarantee. Conditions on the mountain can still change after screening is completed. JVTO may still cancel the hike after screening if volcanic gas, weather, or BBKSDA closures make the route unsafe. The certificate is a condition of access — it does not remove the operational risks of an active volcano environment.

Guests with heart, lung, or circulation conditions are advised to consult their own doctor before booking, regardless of whether screening is required.

---

## Gas Masks — Separate from Screening

Gas masks are provided by JVTO as a standard inclusion on all Ijen-relevant packages. This is independent of and separate from the health-screening process. Whether or not screening is required on your hike date, gas masks are standard equipment on all JVTO Ijen routes.
