## Description Block (150 words)

Mount Bromo sits inside the vast Tengger caldera in East Java — an active stratovolcano surrounded by a ~10 km² plain of volcanic sand known as the Sea of Sand (Pasir Berbisik).

The draw is the Penanjakan sunrise. From the 2,770 m viewpoint, guests watch first light break over the caldera rim with Bromo's smoke plume rising in the foreground. The window is pre-dawn: 03:00–05:00 departure. The 4WD jeep traverse across the caldera floor to get there is not optional logistics — it is the experience.

After sunrise, the route drops to the caldera floor for the crater walk. A short hike or horse ride from the jeep drop-off reaches the rim of the active vent. Pura Luhur Poten, a Hindu temple at the caldera floor, marks the approach.

JVTO holds BBKSDA national-park operator authorization. Guests enter without paperwork. The 4WD jeep is pre-arranged and included — no on-the-day haggling.

## FAQ (AEO-formatted)

**What time does the Bromo tour depart?**
Midnight (00:00) for the standard sunrise window. The pre-dawn drive and 4WD jeep traverse put guests at Penanjakan for first light at approximately 05:00–05:30.

**Is the 4WD jeep included?**
Yes. A private 4WD jeep is pre-arranged and included in all Bromo-relevant packages. For groups larger than 4, additional jeeps are added. There is no on-the-day haggling.

**What if Bromo is closed for volcanic activity?**
JVTO uses a Plan B framework. If BBKSDA closes the crater area, we activate an alternative-route SOP. You are briefed in advance — no day is lost, and the policy is written (not a verbal promise).

**Are tours private or shared?**
100% private. Every booking has a dedicated vehicle, driver, and guide. No shared groups, no join-in tours.

**Is a health certificate required for Bromo?**
No. Health-certificate coordination applies only to Ijen-relevant packages when BBKSDA SE.1658/KSA.9/2024 thresholds require it. Bromo-only packages have no such requirement.
